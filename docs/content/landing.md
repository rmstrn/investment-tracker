### Sub-hero rationale (why this over the v1 triplet «Remembers. Notices. Explains.»)

The v1 draft sub-hero was a 3-word abstract triplet under a metaphor hero («Second Brain…»). Under the v3.1 imperative hero («Ask your portfolio.»), the sub-hero's job changes: the hero already IS the imperative; the sub-hero must (a) answer *«what happens when I ask?»*, (b) name Memoro as the agent doing the remembering/noticing/explaining (not «we»), (c) carry the 3-surface mapping (chat = explains · insights = notices · coach = remembers).

«Just ask.» echoes the hero rhythm. «Memoro remembers / notices / explains» introduces the product name + its three-verb identity in one line. «What you hold / what you'd miss / what it sees» anchors each verb to a concrete object — this is the finance-advisor + user-researcher consensus fix (user voice runs on object-specific verbs, not abstract cognitive ones).

This section hosts the tagline («Second Brain for Your Portfolio») in its locked mid-page role (v3.1 positioning — NOT hero, NOT product name). The narrative tells the user WHY this product uses a memory metaphor — after the functional promise has already landed from the imperative hero above.

### Section header

| Language | Text |
|---|---|
| English (recommended) | **A second brain for your portfolio.** |
| Russian (parallel secondary) | **Второй мозг для твоего портфеля.** |

### Narrative body (~80-100 words English, ~80-100 words Russian)

**English (recommended):**

> Your portfolio lives in seven places. Your dividends are in three inboxes. Your reasons for buying NVDA in 2023 are in a group chat you can't find.
>
> Memoro holds it in one place. Remembers what you hold, across every broker and exchange. Notices what would slip past — a dividend coming, a drawdown forming, a concentration creeping up. Reads patterns in your past trades and shows them to you — without telling you what to do.
>
> One memory across chat, insights, and coach. On your real positions. With sources for every answer.

**Russian (parallel secondary):**

> Твой портфель живёт в семи местах. Дивиденды — в трёх почтовых ящиках. Почему ты купил NVDA в 2023-м — в групповом чате, который ты уже не найдёшь.
>
> Memoro держит всё это в одном месте. Помнит, что у тебя есть — по всем брокерам и биржам. Замечает то, что бы проскользнуло мимо — входящий дивиденд, формирующуюся просадку, подрастающую концентрацию. Читает паттерны в твоих прошлых сделках и показывает их тебе — не говоря, что делать.
>
> Одна память: чат, инсайты, коуч. На твоих реальных позициях. Со ссылками на источник в каждом ответе.

### Narrative rationale

This mid-page block does three jobs the hero + top-of-fold can't do alone:

1. **Resolves the user-researcher semantic-collision flag.** User-researcher §1 evidenced that «second brain for finance» in the adjacent content layer (Jimmy's Journal, Zen Finance AI, LinkedIn pulse) already means *research-notes system*, not *portfolio tracker*. The first paragraph above («Your portfolio lives in seven places…») immediately reframes the metaphor around *aggregation + recall of what you own and what you did*, not around *notes you took on stocks you researched*. This is the disambiguation the user-researcher review asked for — it does heavy-lifting the v1 draft «Remembers. Notices. Explains.» sub-hero could not do.
2. **Maps three surfaces without feature-listing.** «Remembers what you hold → Notices what would slip past → Reads patterns in your past trades» = chat + insights + coach, with the three verbs doing the mapping narratively, not as a bullet list. The sub-hero «remembers/notices/explains» already surfaced this; this narrative reinforces it with specific objects (dividends, drawdowns, concentration, past trades).
3. **Carries «Second Brain» tagline load without letting it eat the hero.** The section header IS the tagline. The narrative supports it. But the imperative hero («Ask your portfolio.») already landed the functional promise, so the metaphor doesn't carry first-impression cognitive load alone.

### Lane A checklist (narrative body)

- [x] No imperative investment advice. «Without telling you what to do» is the explicit Lane A disclaim baked into narrative voice.
- [x] No performance promise. «Forms a drawdown» is observational; not «will recover», not «expected to return».
- [x] No advisor-paternalism. Memoro «shows» patterns; never «advises to avoid them».
- [x] Retrospective framing on Coach. «Reads patterns in your past trades» — not «predicts your next trade». Stays in retrospective-observation, which is outside EU MiFID II + UK FCA personal-recommendation definitions (finance-advisor §3.5).
- [x] Specific, concrete. «Seven places» / «three inboxes» / «group chat» = real user-voice pain (user-researcher §3 fragmentation pain is first-articulated retail pain). Does not lead with abstract cognitive-overload.
