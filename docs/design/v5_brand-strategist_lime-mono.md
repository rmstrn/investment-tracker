**Author:** brand-strategist agent

**Brief:** PO 2026-05-02 directive: drop `--d1-accent-purple` entirely. Provedo becomes a single-brand-color identity — lime carries 100% of brand-primary signal. Purple's identity duties (AI avatar / Premium chip / cohort marker / accent moments) need brand-correct redistribution to lime variations or neutral treatments. v4 synthesis (canvas hue 280° chroma 0.003–0.005, ramp 12/16/19/23%, sage-green + clay-coral at amber register, hybrid model, Variant B grammar) STAYS.

## 1. VERDICT

**SUPPORT — lime as Provedo's monocolor.**

Single-brand-color identity is a meaningful brand stance, not a constraint. PO is correct that purple was not earning its keep — it was carrying *AI-as-other* semantic, which actively conflicts with Magician+Sage archetype where AI is *Provedo's voice*, not a guest faction inside the product. Dropping purple consolidates the identity around one signal. This is brand-strengthening, not brand-flattening.

Conditions on SUPPORT:

1. **Lime gets a 3-tier variant system** (signal / soft / hairline) — see §4. More than one tier is required because lime now carries spotlight + chip + AI mark + filter outline + heatmap ramp; one undifferentiated lime cannot do all that without one of the roles overpowering.
2. **AI avatar moves from filled-purple-tile to hairline-ink mark** (see §5) — making AI a *typographic / structural* distinction rather than a *colored* one. This protects against «AI becomes the brand» trap.
3. **Premium chip becomes a typographic distinction (Mono-uppercase + tighter tracking + ink-deep on lime-soft)** — see §6. Color *and* type signal premium together; type carries the bulk of the load.
4. **Drift-alert micro-mark migrates from purple to clay-coral** — drift = data-attention, not brand-identity. Already covered by `--d1-finance-down` semantic; no new color needed.
5. **Cohort marker drops entirely** — cohort distinctions are surfaced via copy + position, not color. Color was overspending on a low-frequency signal.

## 2. DNA lens on lime-mono

**Magician + Sage primary · Everyman modifier.** v3.1-locked archetype. Read against single-color identity:

**Sage gets stronger.** Sage is restraint, taxonomy, considered choices. Two brand colors (lime + purple) was *more vocabulary* but also *more places to be wrong*. One brand color is harder to misuse, easier to read as «we picked this on purpose». Field Notes' Pitch Black notebook is one accent per series — that discipline reads as *expert curation*, not as *limitation*. Sage anti-position protected: «we are not the color-carnival fintech».

**Magician gets stronger.** Magician is reveal, concentrated moment, the «aha». Two brand colors splits the reveal — when both lime and purple are on screen, neither is THE color. With one color, every lime appearance is a *concentrated-signal moment*. The lime Record Rail tick now reads as «Provedo entered this in the record»; the lime focus-ring reads as «Provedo is paying attention here»; the lime KPI fill reads as «Provedo says look here». Single-channel signal = stronger Magician.

**Everyman holds.** Everyman is approachability. The risk with mono-color is *austerity* — luxury brands use mono to feel exclusive. Provedo cannot afford «exclusive»; the ICP is multi-broker millennials, not HNW. Mitigation: lime at OKLCH 91% L 0.21 C is a *bright, warm, unambiguous* lime — Spotify-loud, not Aesop-quiet. The brightness keeps Everyman alive while the discipline of one-color keeps Sage alive.

**Voice fingerprint composition.** «Provides clarity» = single accent supports cleanly (one color = one place attention goes = clarity). «Notice what you'd miss» = lime *is* the «notice» moment. Without competing purple, lime carries «notice» semantic without dilution. The voice argument lands on lime-mono.

**Lane-A trust-via-restraint.** A single brand color is the strongest possible restraint signal. «We are not advisors selling you complexity» pairs naturally with «we are not a wedding-cake palette selling you novelty». The two restraints reinforce each other — visual restraint *is* trust-restraint, made visible.

**Net:** lime-mono strengthens 3 of 3 archetype components and strengthens both voice fingerprints. Strong DNA support.

## 3. Brand precedent verdict — top 3 references

**1. Spotify (green alone, hue 142° at chroma 0.20+, OKLCH ~78% L).** The closest functional precedent. Spotify's green is loud, unmistakable, carries 100% of brand identity, and lives on a near-black canvas. Premium tier, podcast tier, AI-DJ tier — all surfaced through *typographic* and *structural* distinctions, not through a second brand color. Spotify proved single-loud-color identity at consumer scale (~600M MAU) without brand fatigue. **Teaches Provedo:** loud-green-single-color works at scale; resist the impulse to add a second color when feature surface area expands.

**2. Stripe Dashboard (purple alone, hue ~285° at chroma 0.18, OKLCH ~64% L).** The closest *positioning* precedent. Stripe sells trust to a sophisticated B2B audience, just as Provedo sells trust to a sophisticated retail audience (ICP A is Notion / Obsidian cohort — they recognize Stripe). Stripe's brand-purple appears on CTAs, links, focus-rings, and one signature element (the API-key reveal flash); everything else is grayscale + status colors. The discipline reads as *expert taxonomy*, not as *austerity*. **Teaches Provedo:** the AI-avatar problem is solvable — Stripe doesn't have a "Stripe avatar" inside the product; Stripe *is* the product, and the brand color signs the product's voice. Provedo's AI is Provedo's voice, not a guest entity.

**3. Robinhood (green alone, hue ~140° at chroma 0.20, OKLCH ~76% L).** The closest *category* precedent — financial app, single brand color, retail audience. Robinhood proved green-mono in *finance* (where green has direction-color semantic complications). Their solution: brand-green is *brighter* and *more saturated* than data-up green, so the eye reads them as different despite the hue overlap. **Teaches Provedo:** lime at OKLCH 91% L 0.21 C is *more lightful* than `--d1-finance-up` at 82% L 0.13 C — a 9-L-point gap and a 0.08-C-point gap. The two greens read as *brand-loud* vs *data-quiet*. The discipline matches Robinhood's, executed at the OKLCH level.

**Honourable mentions (not top-3 but referenced):**

- **Coinbase (blue alone)** — proves single-color in finance. Excluded from top 3 because blue-mono is the saturated reflex Provedo specifically rejected (v4 anti-pattern §8: «Discord/GitHub-Dark cool-blue»).
- **Aesop / Le Labo (near-monochrome luxury)** — proves restraint as identity. Excluded because luxury-quiet is Sage-correct but Everyman-broken; Provedo cannot go Aesop-quiet.
- **Field Notes / DDC (single accent per series)** — already cited in v4 §7. Reinforces the discipline; the per-series rotation isn't applicable to a SaaS product with one identity, so demoted to honourable.

**Failure precedents (what Provedo MUST NOT become):**

- **Mailchimp pre-rebrand (yellow + everything)** — yellow as brand, but every feature got its own color tag. Reads as carnival.
- **Slack pre-rebrand (color carnival aubergine + everything)** — same failure mode. Slack's 2019 rebrand collapsed to 4 disciplined brand colors precisely because the carnival was costing recognition.
- **Trello (blue + every-board-its-own-color)** — blue carries no semantic; per-board color carries all the meaning. Brand becomes invisible.

## 4. Lime variant count + brand-correct naming

**Recommendation: 3 variants. Naming convention: semantic-role, not numeric-step.**

```css
[data-style="d1"] {
  /* Lime — brand-primary signal, 3 semantic tiers */

  /* SIGNAL — full-chroma, full-attention. Used for the Provedo-signature moment.
     Record Rail tick · focus-ring · look-here KPI fill · active filter outline.
     This is THE lime; appearances must be earned. */
  --d1-accent-lime-signal: oklch(91% 0.21 117);   /* hex ≈ #DAFF26 */

  /* SOFT — lime washed back, used for fills where signal-lime would shout.
     Premium chip background · AI byline body tint · selection-highlight bg.
     Reads as "lime, but quiet"; same hue, lower chroma, slightly cooler L. */
  --d1-accent-lime-soft: oklch(72% 0.10 117);     /* hex ≈ #B3CC52 */

  /* HAIRLINE — lime as a 1px structural mark only, not a fill.
     Record Rail rule · AI-row left-border · pull-quote frame · table row separator
     (when the row is AI-attributed). Functions as a *line*, never as a *fill*. */
  --d1-accent-lime-hairline: oklch(91% 0.21 117);  /* same OKLCH as -signal,
                                                    * but contract: only used at 1px.
                                                    * Token exists for grep-ability and
                                                    * Tailwind/utility-class clarity. */
}
```

**Why 3, not 2 or 4:**

- **2 variants (signal / soft) is too few.** The hairline use is functionally distinct from both — it's never a fill, never carries weight, only structures. Folding it into `-signal` makes the «1px lime line on dark canvas» look semantically equivalent to the «32px lime KPI fill»; they aren't. Variant B grammar is the system that established hairlines as a first-class structural primitive (the Record Rail, AI-row borders); they need a token name they can grep for.
- **4 variants is overspend.** A 4-tier ramp (`-100/-200/-300/-400`) signals «utility class system» — Tailwind-default reflex. That collides with the brand stance: Provedo is *editorial-curated*, not *utility-grid*. Three tiers signals «we picked these on purpose»; four tiers signals «we generated a scale».
- **Semantic naming, not numeric.** `-signal / -soft / -hairline` reads as designer's intent (each variant has a job). `-100 / -200 / -300` reads as Tailwind import (each variant is a step). The naming convention is itself a brand signal — Provedo CSS reads as *design-system*, not as *utility-soup*.

**Anti-pattern guardrail:** never define `-lime-bright` or `-lime-dark`. «Bright» and «dark» are visual descriptors, not semantic roles. They invite designers to reach for one based on *how it looks on this surface* rather than *what it means*. Semantic naming forces the meaning question to be answered first.

## 5. AI avatar — brand-correct treatment

**Recommendation: hairline-ink mark on dark plate. No filled lime monogram.**

### 5.1 The problem with filled lime «P»

Lime monogram avatar = lime *fills* a circle = lime carries «AI is its own thing». But Provedo's archetype says AI *is* Provedo's voice — not a guest faction. A filled lime avatar makes AI look like an internal sub-brand with its own loud color, which is exactly the brand mistake Stripe-Dashboard avoids by not having a "Stripe avatar" inside Stripe.

Worse: filled lime avatar = the heaviest lime appearance in the product = lime starts meaning «AI» more than «look here». Brand semantic collapses.

### 5.2 The solution — hairline-ink P on dark plate

```text
┌──────────────┐
│  ┌─────┐    │
│  │  P  │    │   ← 32×32 dark plate (oklch(20% 0.003 280) — bg-surface)
│  └─────┘    │      with 1px lime-hairline border
└──────────────┘     and ink-primary "P" mark inside (Geist Mono, 600 weight)
```

```css
.ai-avatar {
  width: 32px;
  height: 32px;
  background: var(--d1-bg-surface);              /* dark plate, blends into canvas */
  border: 1px solid var(--d1-accent-lime-hairline); /* the lime "Provedo signed this" */
  border-radius: 50%;
  display: grid;
  place-items: center;
  font-family: var(--d1-font-mono);
  font-weight: 600;
  font-size: 13px;
  color: var(--d1-text-primary);                 /* ink, not lime */
}
```

### 5.3 Brand-DNA rationale

- **Magician composes:** the *hairline* is the reveal. The lime border is so thin it reads as «Provedo signed this» without screaming «AI here». Concentrated-signal Magician.
- **Sage composes:** ink-on-dark-plate with a lime border is the most restrained possible avatar. No fill, no glow, no animation. Sage anti-position: «we are not the AI-startup with the throbbing purple orb».
- **Everyman composes:** the «P» monogram remains legible (Mono 13px on dark plate, ink-primary text = AAA contrast). Approachability preserved.
- **Lane-A trust composes:** the avatar doesn't shout «AI THINKS YOU SHOULD…»; it whispers «Provedo took this note». Information/education tone.

### 5.4 Rejected alternatives

- **Filled lime monogram:** rejected per §5.1 — overuses lime semantic, makes AI look like a sub-brand.
- **Symbolic mark (Record Rail tick repeated):** rejected — the Record Rail tick is already a structural element; reusing it as an avatar dilutes the «Provedo entered this» semantic. One job per mark.
- **No avatar (1px lime byline only):** rejected as the *primary* treatment, but **adopted as the COMPACT VARIANT.** When AI rows are listed in dense surfaces (sidebar feed, condensed insights list), the 32×32 avatar is too heavy — drop to a single `--d1-accent-lime-hairline` left-border on the row, no avatar. Two treatments (full avatar + hairline-only) handle two density regimes.

## 6. Premium chip — brand-correct treatment

**Recommendation: typographic-first + lime-soft fill. Not color-only.**

### 6.1 The construction

```text
┌──────────────┐
│  PREMIUM     │   ← Geist Mono, 11px, 600 weight, +0.08em tracking, UPPERCASE
└──────────────┘     on var(--d1-accent-lime-soft) fill
                     with 1px var(--d1-accent-lime-hairline) border
                     ink-deep text color (var(--d1-text-ink))
                     6px horizontal padding, 3px vertical padding, 4px border-radius
```

```css
.chip-premium {
  font-family: var(--d1-font-mono);
  font-size: 11px;
  font-weight: 600;
  letter-spacing: 0.08em;
  text-transform: uppercase;
  color: var(--d1-text-ink);
  background: var(--d1-accent-lime-soft);
  border: 1px solid var(--d1-accent-lime-hairline);
  padding: 3px 6px;
  border-radius: 4px;
}
```

### 6.2 Brand-DNA rationale

- **Mono-uppercase + tracking** = «designed-spec-sheet» typography. NYT-charts data-table headers, Field Notes inventory labels, Aaron Draplin trade-mark notation. Reads as *editorial taxonomy*, not as *bling chip*.
- **Lime-soft fill (not lime-signal)** = «this is premium, but Provedo doesn't shout about it». Restraint = Sage. The soft fill carries «set-apart» semantic without overspending the signal-lime budget. (Signal-lime appearances must be earned; chip backgrounds aren't earned.)
- **1px lime-hairline border** = the «Provedo signed this» tier signature. Same hairline as Record Rail and AI avatar; vocabulary consistency.
- **Ink-deep text** = AAA contrast (~13:1 on lime-soft). Legibility = Lane-A clarity.

The chip carries «premium» through **three reinforcing signals** (type-spec + soft-fill + hairline-border), so dropping color alone would still leave type carrying the load. This is the redundancy that makes a single-brand-color system survive accessibility constraints — type-spec reaches color-blind users that color-alone cannot.

### 6.3 Rejected alternatives

- **Lime-signal fill + ink text:** rejected — overuses signal-lime, competes with KPI / focus-ring / Record Rail for the «look here» semantic. Premium chip is not a «look here» moment.
- **Outline-only (1px lime border, no fill, no special type):** rejected — too quiet to register as «paid tier». Premium chip needs to *register* as a status, not just blend in.
- **No color (Mono-uppercase + ink-on-bg-surface only):** rejected as the chip treatment, but **kept as the implementation-pattern reference.** A future *secondary* chip (e.g., «BETA», «NEW», «AI») can use the same Mono-uppercase + tracking treatment with no fill — the type-spec is the brand-mark, the chip variants differ in fill weight only. This gives Provedo a chip taxonomy: signal-fill (Premium), no-fill (Beta/New/AI), ink-fill (system-status).

## 8. Owned-able verdict — is `oklch(91% 0.21 117)` distinctive enough?

**Verdict: YES — distinctive enough for Provedo to own as monocolor.**

### 8.1 Lime-yellow brand survey

Brands using a lime-or-yellow-green at high lightness (≥85% L) and high chroma (≥0.18 C):

- **Acid League (food/beverage)** — uses a lime-yellow at ~92% L, ~0.20 C, hue ~110°. Closest competitor in OKLCH space. Different category (consumer beverage vs fintech), different audience (specialty grocery vs portfolio retail). Trademark and category cleanly separate.
- **Whole Foods (Amazon-era)** — uses a green at ~70% L, ~0.18 C, hue ~140°. *Greener* and *less light* than Provedo's lime. Reads as «leaf», not as «electric». Cleanly separable.
- **Tesco Clubcard / various supermarket lime** — usually ~80% L, ~0.16 C, hue ~125°. Less light, less chromatic. Reads as «retail-cheap-lime», which is exactly NOT Provedo's voice. Reads as a different color register entirely.
- **DocuSign («docusign yellow»)** — ~88% L, ~0.18 C, hue ~95°. *Yellower* (closer to amber). Different hue family.
- **Snapchat (yellow)** — ~95% L, ~0.20 C, hue ~92°. *Pure yellow*, no green. Different hue family (25° apart).
- **DJI / various tech-product lime** — varies; usually used as accent on hardware, not brand-mono. Not relevant precedent.

**Closest actual conflict:** Acid League. But (a) different category, (b) Acid League uses lime as one of three brand colors (lime + black + cream), not as monocolor, and (c) Provedo's hue 117 vs Acid League's 110 is 7° apart — perceptually distinguishable in side-by-side. No infringement risk; minor recognition risk in tech-savvy ICP A who shop at specialty grocery, but they'll read «Provedo lime» as different the moment they see Provedo's typography + dark canvas (Acid League is light-canvas).

### 8.2 Why hue 117 at L 91 C 0.21 is *more* distinctive than typical lime

Two reasons:

**(1) The L 91 + C 0.21 combination is unusually high-lightness AND high-chroma.** Most «lime» brand colors compromise on one — Whole Foods drops L to 70 to keep C at 0.18 (deep-leaf); Snapchat drops C to 0.18 to keep L at 95 (pure-electric-yellow). Provedo holds *both* — high L AND high C — which OKLCH-mathematically means **maximum visual energy.** The color is at the «edge of the gamut» for that hue. Reads as electric-lime, not as leaf-green or pastel-lime. Distinctive.

**(2) The hue 117 sits in a no-man's-land between «green» (140°) and «yellow» (90°).** Most brand colors anchor on a named color (green, yellow, blue). Hue 117 is *between* names — neither a green nor a yellow but a *yellow-green*, which is a less-claimed territory. The cognitive load of naming Provedo's color is slightly higher («it's like a chartreuse… no, it's like a lime… no, it's…»), and that *unclaimable-by-a-noun* quality is itself a brand asset. Coined-but-rooted, just like the product name (Memoro / Provedo). The color and the name share a brand-DNA pattern: both refuse to be commodity-described.

### 8.3 Mono-color durability test

The Robinhood-Spotify-Stripe pattern proves single-loud-color brands survive feature expansion when:
- The color has a *signature use case* that anchors recognition (Spotify = play-button green; Stripe = CTA-purple; Robinhood = up-arrow + brand-mark green).
- The color is **never the only signal** for any function — it's always paired with type or position.
- The color is *more saturated* than the data colors it sits next to (so brand-loud and data-quiet stay distinct).

Provedo passes all three:
- Signature anchor: Record Rail tick (the lime hairline that signs every AI-attributed entry).
- Never-only: every lime use is paired with type (KPI numeral), position (left-border attribution), or shape (focus-ring on input).
- More saturated: lime at L 91 C 0.21 is +9 L points and +0.08 C points louder than `--d1-finance-up` at L 82 C 0.13.

**Owned-able. Confident.**

## 9. Anti-pattern Provedo MUST NOT do in lime-mono

**The single biggest brand failure mode for v5: «Lime as decoration — lime borders on everything».**

When you remove a brand color, the temptation is to compensate by *applying the remaining color more often*. That's the failure mode that broke Mailchimp pre-rebrand (yellow on every feature) and Trello (blue + every-board-its-own-color). The discipline that saves lime-mono is: **fewer lime appearances, not more.**

### 9.1 Hard rules

- **Never let `--d1-accent-lime-signal` appear more than 3 times on a single screen.** If a 4th appearance is requested, demote one to `-soft` or `-hairline`. Signal is for *concentrated moments*; concentration breaks at 4+.
- **Never use lime as a decorative border around generic containers.** Lime-hairline is reserved for AI-attribution rows, Record Rail rules, and pull-quote frames. Generic card borders use `--d1-edge-stroke`, not lime. Decorative-lime breaks Record Rail's earned semantic.
- **Never animate lime on hover.** Hover is a NO-OP per Variant B grammar. Lime that pulses, glows, or scales on hover collapses Sage restraint. Press-tier (active state) is the only state where lime can change weight, and even then by ≤1 L point.
- **Never let lime become a sub-brand color.** No «Provedo Pro lime» variant, no «Provedo AI lime» variant, no «cohort lime». The 3 variants in §4 are the system. Adding a 4th tier-by-feature breaks the «we picked 3» discipline and starts the carnival.
- **Never pair lime with another saturated brand color** (no lime + electric-blue, no lime + magenta, no lime + cyan). Mono means mono. The amber + sage + clay data-direction triad is the *only* other chromatic register; data colors stay quiet (L 82 C 0.13) and never compete with lime.
- **Never use lime as the only signal for any state change.** Color-blind users (deuteranopia ~5% male population) read lime and sage-green as similar hues. Pair lime with type-spec (Mono-caps for premium), position (left-border for AI), or shape (focus-ring vs fill) so the signal survives without color.

### 9.2 The «luxury austerity» trap

Aesop / Le Labo prove mono-color works at luxury. Provedo cannot inherit that aesthetic — Aesop's mono-color is paired with *quiet typography* and *wide whitespace*, which signals *expensive*. Provedo's lime is bright, the type is Geist (utilitarian-modern, not luxurious-serif), the canvas is dense (multi-broker dashboard, lots of data). If lime-mono is paired with *more whitespace + quieter type*, the brand drifts toward austerity — wrong audience signal for ICP A (multi-broker millennials).

**The check:** when the v5 design is shown to PO, the gut-read should be «lime is the loud spot in a serious system», not «this looks expensive and exclusive». If gut-read drifts to «expensive», pull whitespace tighter, push data density up, and add one more functional element to the screen.

### 9.3 The «AI-as-color» relapse

Without purple, every product feature that *was* purple-coded (AI byline, AI avatar, AI-attribution) will be tempted to relocate to lime. The discipline (per §7 + §5) is that AI doesn't need a color — it needs a *structural* signature (the lime hairline border on AI-rows, the dark-plate avatar). If lime starts showing up *next to AI text* as a tag or chip, AI has stolen the brand color and the brand semantic collapses again, just with a different hue. **AI gets structure, not color.**
