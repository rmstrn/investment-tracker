## 2. Pricing archetypes observed

| Archetype | Example products | Characteristics |
|---|---|---|
| **Freemium with feature gate** | Sharesight, Snowball, Getquin, Simply Wall St, Tickeron, WallStreetZen, PortfolioPilot, Public.com | Free tier has meaningful utility; paid unlocks unlimited holdings, advanced analytics, or AI |
| **Freemium with usage gate (AI tokens/prompts)** | **Fiscal.ai** (10 → 100 prompts/mo at $24) | Modern pattern for AI features; caps prompts/messages |
| **Freemium OSS (self-host free, pay for cloud)** | Wealthfolio (Connect), Ghostfolio (Premium) | Privacy-conscious segment; zero-cost for self-hosters |
| **Trial + paid only** | Copilot Money ($95/yr), Monarch ($99.99/yr), Fey ($300/yr), Kubera ($250/yr), Mezzi ($299/yr) | No free tier; 7-30 day trial then paid |
| **Flat single-tier paid** | Alinea ($120/yr), Fey ($300/yr) | No complex gating; one-price-for-everything |
| **Broker-subsidized (free subscription, revenue from spread/PFOF)** | Moomoo, Bobby/RockFlow, Public.com (base) | Product is the brokerage; AI is layered free |
| **AUM-based advisory** | Farther | Percentage fee, advisor-led |
| **Flat-fee advisory (no AUM)** | Range ($2,655/yr), Mezzi ($299-1,499/yr) | Subscription replaces % AUM |
| **Full premium — no free, only paid tiers** | Mezzi, Fey, Kubera | Retention + intent quality tradeoff |

## 3. Feature-gate patterns (what Free tier typically gates vs gives)

Sampled across 10 freemium competitors (Sharesight, Snowball, Getquin, Simply Wall St, Tickeron, WallStreetZen, PortfolioPilot, Public, Gainify, Wealthfolio):

**Typically given on Free tier:**
- 1 portfolio with 10 holdings cap (Sharesight, Snowball, Simply Wall St)
- Unlimited broker connections (Getquin, PortfolioPilot)
- Basic charts + allocation view
- Some form of limited chat prompts per month (Fiscal.ai 10/mo, Fey trial 20)
- Free research tool or stock reports (Gainify, Simply Wall St 5/mo)

**Typically gated on paid:**
- Unlimited portfolios + holdings
- Historical data beyond 90 days
- Tax reports (especially multi-jurisdiction)
- Benchmark comparison
- Advanced screening / multi-period reporting
- **AI assistant beyond limited access** (PortfolioPilot Gold has "limited", Platinum unlocks "unlimited")
- Scenario modeling (PortfolioPilot Platinum+, Getquin Wealth)
- Custom alerts
- Export / API access
- Priority support

**Rare on Free tier:**
- Unlimited AI chat (only Wealthfolio OSS, Origin intro, Moomoo as broker)

## 4. Our proposed pricing vs the field

Proposed (from `02_POSITIONING.md`):

| Tier | Price | Annual equiv |
|---|---|---|
| Free | $0 | $0 |
| Plus | ~$8-10/mo | ~$96-120/yr |
| Pro | ~$20/mo | ~$240/yr |

### Where each tier sits

**Free $0:**
- Aligns with Sharesight, Snowball, Getquin, Simply Wall St, Gainify, PortfolioPilot, Public, Wealthfolio, Ghostfolio
- **Crowded market** — 9+ competitors offer free tier. Free is table-stakes in retail tracking, not a moat.
- **Our gate (2 accounts, 90-day history, 5 AI msg/day, 1 insight/week)** is slightly tighter than Getquin Free (unlimited connections) or PortfolioPilot Free (unlimited net-worth tracking, scenario planning). On that comparison, our Free feels restrictive.

**Plus $8-10/mo ($96-120/yr):**
- Sits below: Copilot ($95/yr), Snowball Starter ($79.99/yr — cheaper), Sharesight Starter ($84/yr — cheaper), Getquin Premium ($97/yr — same)
- Sits at the same anchor as **Copilot + Snowball + Monarch Core + Getquin Premium** — crowded 4-way tie around $80-120/yr
- **Above**: Nothing free (except OSS)
- **Fair comp:** Getquin Premium at $97/yr includes AI-powered portfolio analysis — direct match to our Plus claim
- **Risk:** Anchored in budget-tracker / dividend-tracker tier price-wise, suggesting retail buyers perceive this as "advanced tracker" not "AI advisor"
- **Opportunity:** Usage-gate pattern (like Fiscal.ai 10 → 100 prompts at $24) is underused. Consider "Free 5 msg/day, Plus unlimited chat" as a cleaner gate than account-count gate

**Pro $20/mo ($240/yr):**
- Sits exactly at: **PortfolioPilot Gold ($20/mo annual, $240/yr)**, Tickeron Model ($20/mo → $240/yr), ChatGPT Plus ($240/yr), WallStreetZen ($234/yr), Albert ($240/yr), Kubera Essentials ($250/yr)
- **Very crowded $240/yr anchor** — 6+ competitors
- PortfolioPilot Gold at $240/yr gives AI recommendations + tax optimization + hidden fee detection + limited AI assistant access — direct feature-match to our Pro tier
- **Risk:** Selling against PortfolioPilot at identical price, they have 40K users and $30B AUM of proof — we need differentiation on experience not just price

### Gaps in the market we're NOT addressing

- **$30-50/mo tier** (Fey $30/mo, Composer $32/mo, PortfolioPilot Platinum $49/mo, Mezzi $50/mo equiv) — power-user / near-HNW retail. We don't have this tier.
- **Flat-fee advisory replacement ($1,500-3,000/yr)** — Mezzi, Range territory. Not our play.

### Pricing recommendation flags for Navigator

1. **Free is table-stakes.** 9+ competitors have it. We must compete on feature quality within Free, not on "having" it.
2. **Plus $8-10 sits in a crowded 4-way cluster** around $80-120/yr (Copilot, Snowball, Monarch, Getquin). Need differentiation beyond price — AI-first UX at this tier is our play.
3. **Pro $20 matches PortfolioPilot Gold exactly.** They have $30B AUM proof and "12,000+ institutions" aggregation. Direct head-to-head. Consider: either undercut at $15/mo, or differentiate via UX + EU coverage + pure-tracker positioning (no advisor-y tone).
4. **Missing tier risk:** Our $0 → $10 → $20 stops at PortfolioPilot Gold level. We have no answer for power users willing to pay $30-50 (Fey, Composer, Mezzi territory). If ICP A ($20-100K portfolio) includes sophisticated users, they may jump to PortfolioPilot Platinum ($49/mo unlocks unlimited AI + scenario sim + AI equity research) — our Pro doesn't compete.
5. **Usage-gate is underused and modern** (Fiscal.ai prompts). Our Free "5 AI msg/day" is a good start; consider making Free/Plus distinction primarily about AI quota, not account/history counts.
6. **Naming signals**: Most competitors go Free / Starter / Standard / Premium or Free / Gold / Platinum / Pro. Our Free / Plus / Pro is clean but "Plus" overlaps with Public.com Premium and Monarch Plus — minor conflict, not blocking.

## 5. What free tiers say about business model intent

| Competitor | Free-tier strategy | Reveal about intent |
|---|---|---|
| **PortfolioPilot** | Very generous free (scenario planning, net worth, unlimited accounts) | Free-to-hook-then-upsell-AI. The "real" AI is gated behind Gold/Platinum. |
| **Getquin** | Free has unlimited connections, paid gates AI + analytics | Similar: connections are cheap, AI is the monetization lever |
| **Snowball / Sharesight** | Free gates holdings count (10-holding limit) | Classic SaaS feature-count gate; no AI play |
| **Monarch / Copilot / Fey** | No free tier (trial only) | Commits users early; signals "premium" positioning |
| **Mezzi / Kubera** | Paid only, no trial-free | Highest-intent user pool; smaller TAM, higher ARPU |
| **Moomoo / Bobby / Public** | "Free" subscription (broker revenue model) | Users ARE the monetization (order flow, spreads, margin lending) |
| **Wealthfolio / Ghostfolio** | OSS free + optional cloud sub | Privacy segment; monetize convenience not capability |

**Implication for us:** Our Free tier's job should be proving the AI-chat magic, not unlocking tracking functionality. The "wow moment" must happen inside 5 msg/day.
