## §2. Per-test notes

- **T1 hero (8).** «Ask Provedo about your portfolio» — phonetics survive cleanly, 3-syl /pro.ˈve.do/, no consonant clusters, RU «Прове́до» 3-syl Cyrillic-clean with stable stress. Reads as named-agent with no semantic collision (vs Lucri 6 where the Latin-profit decode interfered). Half-point off vs Heed/Glint (8) because at first read on landing, English reader without etymology might pre-decode «provedo» faintly as Spanish/Portuguese/Italian — neutral wash, not negative. Bilingual edge: Russian reader decodes «Провéдо» = phonetic «проведу» on first read, which **adds** a meaning-layer rather than collides («ask the one who will lead you through»). Net positive in RU.

- **T2 tagline (8 averaged).** Two variants tested:
  - **Canonical** — «Provedo — Second Brain for Your Portfolio» / «Provedo: твой второй мозг для портфеля» — score **7**. Reads cleanly. No collision (vs Lucri 3 «profit-brain collision»). Soft note: «Provedo» (provide/foresee) and «second brain» (memory) don't collide but also don't reinforce — both Sage-coded, but in different sub-registers (provision/foresight vs memory/recall). Acceptable parallel.
  - **Bilingual native variant** — «Provedo проведёт через твой портфель» — score **9**. This is the unique discovered slot: in Russian the name itself becomes a grammatical subject of its own etymology («Provedo will lead through»). For Russian readers, this single-line tagline does what the entire mid-page narrative does in English — it establishes the product promise via the name. Memoro can't do this (Memoro is a verb in Latin not Russian); Lucri can't do this; Heed/Glint/Span can't do this. Genuine bilingual advantage, not a stretched interpretation.
  - Averaged to **8**. PO can decide which variant to ship, but the bilingual variant is unique-to-Provedo asset.

- **T3 email subject (6).** «Provedo noticed something in your portfolio.» — fits ≤50 char (47 chars EN). Surface-reads as observation-agent. The faint hitch: «Provedo» phonetically pre-loads «provide» in EN reader's ear → subtle semantic mismatch with «noticed» (provider vs observer). Not a collision like Lucri (4: profit collides with memory) — more like a register-blur. RU «Provedo заметил» — gender-marked masculine «заметил» works because «провéдо» pattern is masculine-coded by default in Russian phonotactics (ends in -о, but male-named-agent register dominates: «Memoro заметил», «Provedo заметил» both read masculine without strain). 6 not 7 because EN provider/observer micro-blur is genuine if low-stakes. RU side scores higher in isolation but blends in the average.

- **T4 microcopy (5).** Two sub-tests:
  - «Provedo reads patterns in your past trades» — Lane A retrospective frame.   - **«Ask Provedo»** chat-input button (`btn.ask_memoro` key in `microcopy.md` §1) — highest-frequency string in the product. «Ask Provedo» reads cleanly first time, but at high repetition (100x/day) the name's 3-syllable length adds slight friction vs «Ask Memoro» (3-syl too but ending vowel softer) and especially vs «Ask Heed» (1-syl, button-shaped). This is a marginal performance cost, not a register failure. Compare RU «Спросить Provedo» — 6 syllables total which is OK for chat-input but tests the upper char budget for `btn.ask_memoro` (RU max 30 chars: «Спросить Provedo» = 16 chars, fine).
  - 5 averaged. Repetition-stress is the real concern — name length is on the long side for a button label that fires every chat session.

- **T5 verb-form (8 averaged, RU-strong).** This is where Provedo opens a slot no R20-R32 candidate before it could occupy:

Same failure mode as Lucri (3) and Memoro (4). EN colloquial «to Provedo» = «to provide»? Uncomfortable; «provide» is already an English verb without rebranding, so «Provedo that» reads as marketing-forced.

The verb «провести / проводить» (to lead through, to guide) is native, productive, and is the exact 1st-person-singular conjugation that the name encodes. Russian reader produces these forms by default; no contortion required.

## §3. Lane A regulatory gradient (asymmetric — the load-bearing finding)

This is the most consequential section. Provedo's etymology creates **opposite gradients in EN and RU**, and EN gradient needs active editorial discipline.

### RU gradient — neutral-positive

«Провести / проводить» in Russian = «lead through, guide, accompany, conduct (as in metro line passes through a station)». **Neutral Sage-archetype verb.** No advice-register payload.

Examples that emerge naturally in RU copy:
- «Provedo проведёт тебя через дивиденды» — neutral guide-through framing. Lane A clean.
- «Provedo проведёт через просадку» — observational accompaniment. Lane A clean.
- «Provedo проведёт через твой портфель» — primary metaphor, Lane A clean.

The RU gradient is **on-product**: «lead through / guide through / accompany» is exactly the Sage-archetype voice the locked positioning calls for. The name reinforces what the product does without crossing into advice register.

### EN gradient — concerning

«Provide» in English has a stable collocation pattern that pulls toward advisor-adjacent registers. Examples that emerge naturally with Provedo brand:

- **«Provedo provides clarity on your portfolio»** — borderline. «Provide clarity» is generic SaaS-speak, low risk but flat.
- **«Provedo provides insights on your dividends»** — borderline. «Provide insights» reads as financial-content-marketing register; not advice per se but adjacent.
- **«Provedo provides recommendations on rebalancing»** — **Lane A violation.** «Provide recommendations» is investment-advice EU MiFID II personal-recommendation territory directly. This sentence WOULD be written by a future PR/growth/contractor copy-writer naturally because the name pulls there.
- **«Provedo provides what you need to know about your portfolio»** — borderline. Reads paternalistic; «what you need to know» is advisor-paternalism (Lane A explicit anti-pattern).
- **«Provedo provides fiduciary-grade insight»** — would never be written by content-lead but **could** be written by a marketing contractor. Crosses Lane A.

**Asymmetry summary:** RU gradient = on-product (Sage/guide). EN gradient = downhill toward advisor-paternalism (provider/recommender). 

Compare to prior winners on this axis:
- Memoro: gradient on-product both languages (memory neutral in both).
- Lucri: gradient anti-product both languages (profit collides with Lane A both sides).
- Heed: gradient on-product both languages (observation neutral in both, though Heed is harder for RU readers to parse at all).
- **Provedo: gradient on-product RU, anti-product EN.** Unique split.

### What this means for editorial discipline

If launch is English-day-1 (per current positioning lock), Provedo requires a content-lead-owned **anti-«provide» discipline** as permanent part of `BRAND_VOICE/`. Specifically, ban these collocations from any future EN copy:
1. «Provedo provides advice / recommendations / suggestions»
2. «Provedo provides what you should do»
3. «Provedo provides insights on what to buy/sell/rebalance»
4. «Provedo provides fiduciary / advisor / professional [anything]»
5. Any «Provedo provides [normative-action]» pattern

Allowed «provide» collocations (factual/observational):
- «Provedo provides sources for every answer»
- «Provedo provides one feed across all brokers»
- (Note: even these pull mildly; preferable pattern is to use Memoro-family verbs — surfaces, holds, shows, cites — and avoid «provide» as Provedo's verb-stem entirely except where unavoidable.)

**Recommended discipline:** treat «provide» like a watchword. Any line where Provedo is subject of «provide» triggers content-lead second-look. Cost: low (content-lead gating), recurring (every future copy line), but manageable IF the discipline is documented up-front in BRAND_VOICE before any external-copy writer touches the brand.

This is materially less expensive than Lucri's profit-gradient (which pulled across **all** verbs, not just one stem). Provedo's gradient is concentrated on one verb-stem and one English-surface; Lucri's gradient was diffuse and cross-language.

## §4. Bilingual copy advantage (unique to Provedo)

No other R20-R32 candidate has this. The advantage breaks down into three concrete mechanics:

### 4.1. Name-as-tagline in Russian
- «Provedo проведёт через твой портфель» — single-line tagline that requires no explanation. Russian reader parses it as: «[name] will lead [you] through your portfolio.» Hero + tagline collapsed into one line.
- Memoro can't do this in Russian («Memoro помнит что у тебя есть» is fine but the name doesn't morphologically conjugate into the verb).
- This means: for the post-launch RU wave, Provedo's hero / sub-hero / tagline could be different from EN — denser, more lyrical, name-as-poem.

### 4.2. Wordplay slots in copy
- «Доверь Provedo провести тебя через дивиденды» (entrust Provedo to lead you through dividends) — natural wordplay on name = verb.
- «Provedo поможет провести через волатильность» (Provedo will help you go through volatility) — natural.
- «Когда не знаешь что делать с просадкой — Provedo проведёт» (when you don't know what to do with a drawdown — Provedo will lead [you] through) — strong tagline candidate.
- These read as natural Russian, not forced wordplay (vs Memoro «Memoro мемо-something» which doesn't conjugate).

### 4.3. Verb-form colloquialism in Russian product voice
- «Меня Provedo проведёт» (Provedo will guide me) — natural.
- «Давай я Provedo попрошу» (let me ask Provedo) — natural.
- «Provedo, проведи меня через дивиденды» (Provedo, lead me through dividends) — natural direct-address.
- These give the Russian product voice slots Memoro couldn't fill. Once the product expands to RU-content surfaces (e.g. RU email subjects, RU social, RU help-center copy), the name carries narrative weight by itself.

### 4.4. Quantification

For the **Russian copy surface specifically**, Provedo gives ~40-50% more natural collocation slots than Memoro. The name itself does roughly half the work that brand-narrative copy normally does. This compresses copy budget on the RU side meaningfully.

For the **English copy surface**, Provedo gives roughly the same slots as Memoro (3-syl named-agent) minus the «provide» discipline tax. So EN-side: roughly equivalent to Memoro with a small discipline overhead. RU-side: substantially better than any prior candidate.

**This is a genuine bilingual advantage, not a stretch.** It does not make Provedo an obvious winner over Heed (which has the simpler 1-syl button-shape advantage in EN); it does make Provedo a meaningfully different copy-instrument with Russian-side capabilities the rest of the pool doesn't have.

## §5. Verdict: WARN

Provedo passes copy-fit (avg 7.0, 3rd-best of pool) **with two specific caveats** that are surface-fixable but require explicit acknowledgment before lock:

### Why WARN (not SUPPORT)
The EN-side «provide → recommend» gradient is a real Lane A risk that requires permanent content-lead discipline. It is materially less risky than Lucri's profit-gradient (which I rejected outright at avg 4.2), but it is more risky than Memoro / Heed / Glint (all of which carry zero anti-product gradient on either language).

### Why WARN (not REJECT)
1. T1 (8), T2-bilingual (9), T5-RU (9) are genuinely strong, not generously scored.
2. The bilingual advantage at §4 is unique and substantial for the post-launch RU wave.
3. The EN gradient is concentrated on one verb-stem (`provide`) and addressable via documented BRAND_VOICE discipline. Cost is bounded and low-recurring.
4. Provedo at 7.0 ranks third behind Heed (8.0) and ahead of the R20-R26 top tier (Glint/Span/Arden 7.4) — substantially above Memoro/Lucri reference floor (4.2).

### Conditions for SUPPORT (lift WARN to lock)
1. PO commits to maintaining a permanent `docs/product/BRAND_VOICE/provedo-en-discipline.md` (or equivalent) banning the «Provedo provides [advice/recommendation/normative]» collocation pattern.
2. Content-lead owns a recurring audit of public EN copy surfaces (landing, ads, social, blog, partnership content) for the «provide» pattern, especially when external/contractor writers are involved.
3. PO accepts that EN T5 verb-form is a delayed-value / RU-only asset — does not contribute to English-day-1 acquisition; only helps once RU surfaces ship.

If PO accepts these three conditions, WARN → SUPPORT. If PO does not want the recurring discipline cost, the rational alternative is Heed (R28, 8.0, no gradient on either language).

## §6. Critical findings

- **Bilingual advantage is real and unique.** RU listeners decode «прове́до» = «проведу» natively. T2-bilingual (9), T5-RU (9). No other R20-R32 candidate has this slot.
- **EN «provide» gradient is the load-bearing concern.** Same axis-of-failure as Lucri (gradient toward off-product framing), but more concentrated (single verb-stem) and lower amplitude. Surface-fixable via documented discipline.
- **Asymmetric Lane A risk.** RU gradient on-product (Sage/guide); EN gradient anti-product (provide → recommend). Editorial discipline must be EN-side specifically.
- **Name length on chat button.** «Ask Provedo» (3-syl) is at upper-end of comfortable button-shape repetition. Compare Heed (1-syl) for strict button-shape advantage. Marginal, not deal-breaking.
- **T3 email subject register-blur.** Subtle provider/observer mismatch in «Provedo noticed». Lower than Lucri's profit-collision; higher than Memoro's neutral. Acceptable.
- **Provedo (7.0) ranks third behind Heed (8.0) and ahead of Glint/Span/Arden (7.4)** — meaningfully better than Memoro/Lucri reference (4.2).
- **EN T5 verb-form does not work** (4). Slack/Notion-style «let me Provedo that» doesn't conjugate. Does not block lock; just removes one micro-asset for English colloquial.

## §7. Recommendation

**WARN with clear path to SUPPORT.** Lock Provedo IF PO commits to:
1. Permanent EN `provide`-collocation discipline documented in BRAND_VOICE before any external EN copy writer is hired.
2. Recurring content-lead audit of public EN surfaces for the gradient pattern.
3. Acceptance that EN T5 verb-form is a delayed RU-side asset, not an EN day-1 contribution.

Otherwise, the rational alternative is Heed (R28 winner, 8.0 copy-fit, no gradient discipline cost). Provedo's bilingual advantage is real but only realizes value at RU-launch wave; if RU is sufficiently delayed in actual launch sequencing, the discipline tax may not be worth the deferred upside.

If PO insists on Provedo without conditions: ship anyway, but understand that the EN-side discipline cost will manifest as content-lead friction on every external copy hire and every PR/growth contractor for the life of the product. That cost is bounded; it is not zero.
