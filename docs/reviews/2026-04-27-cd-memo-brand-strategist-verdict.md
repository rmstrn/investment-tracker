## §2. CD's memo, mapped to existing strategic-posture taxonomy (Q1)

**Claim under test:** CD's «receipts as thesis» is a 4th option missed by the synthesis OR a refinement of Option A.

**My finding: neither. It is a structural-axis move, orthogonal to A/B/C/D.**

The Phase-2 synthesis distinguishes A/B/C along a **«show vs tell vs refuse»** axis (R1 §6 frame): trust editorial *show* / trust product *tell* / trust disciplined *refuse*. All three are postural — they declare what kind of brand-statement the page is making. D defers the postural commitment.

CD's «receipts as thesis» does not declare a postural stance on show/tell/refuse. It declares a **visual-system principle**: «the chrome must visually instantiate the product's actual epistemic behavior — every claim cites itself, every chart is a footnote.» That is a constraint on **how** the page communicates, regardless of **which** posture the page adopts.

To make this concrete, CD's «receipt-system» is compatible with all four of A/B/C/D:
- A + receipts = editorial Sage where the editorial mid-page itself has a visible source-line and the prose carries footnote chrome.
- B + receipts = audience-named hero with receipt-styled scope-list sub and source-line on the broker marquee.
- C + receipts = negation hero where the affirmation closer reads as a source-cited assertion («Provedo will not tell you what to buy. — Lane A discipline. — see disclosures»).
- D + receipts = current hero + universal patches + receipt-chrome treatment of the demo-tab content + sources-strip section + footer disclaimer Layer 1.

The synthesis missed this because the synthesizer was looking for the next strategic-posture commitment, not the next visual-system commitment. The synthesizer's taxonomy correctly enumerates posture options; CD adds a chrome principle that lives at a different layer.

**Implication for option-set framing:** the correct re-framing is not «pick one of A/B/C/D» — it is «pick a posture (A/B/C/D) AND pick a chrome system (CD-receipts vs current-chrome-extended).» These are independently selectable. I recommend: **D (posture) × CD-receipts-restrained (chrome)**.

## §4. C-window timing question for «receipts as thesis» (Q3)

My prior verdict said C-window opens after one of three trigger events: brand-press / 1000+ visitors / ICP-A inbound. **Same logic for receipts? My answer: no — different timing logic, because the structural axis is different.**

C-window timing (negation-led hero) was gated on triggers because:
1. **Negation copy needs a referent.** «Provedo is not a robo-advisor» reads defensive without «who are you?» being already-answered. Pre-distribution = no referent.
2. **Negation as hero is postural commitment.** Walking it back signals brand-confusion. Postural commitments compound under distribution; without distribution, they sit as taste-bets.
3. **Negation reframes the visitor relationship.** Pre-data, you don't know if the visitor parses negation as «finally, an honest tool» or as «why is this thing complaining about itself?»

Receipt-chrome doesn't carry these failure modes:
1. **Receipt-chrome doesn't claim anything about Provedo's reputation.** It instantiates Provedo's actual product behavior (the AI cites sources, the disclaimers are layered, the demo content is dated and traceable). The chrome reads as: «this is what the product does, here's the evidence.» That works pre-distribution because it's **showing, not claiming**.
2. **Receipt-chrome is not postural.** It's typographic discipline. Walking it back doesn't read as brand-confusion — it reads as «v1 of the design.» Strategic reversibility is high.
3. **Receipt-chrome does not depend on visitor pre-knowledge.** A cold visitor lands, sees citation-styled chrome on the demo content + a sources-strip after the testimonials + a layered disclaimer in the footer, and parses: «this product is precise about its evidence.» That parse works without prior brand-awareness — it works *because* of the visitor's general expectation that receipts mean honesty.

**Receipt-chrome pays off pre-alpha.** It is structural-not-positional, exactly as the dispatch brief frames the question. It does not require trigger events. The only constraint is content-fidelity: the demo content must actually carry real-feeling specific dollar amounts, tickers, dates, source labels (which the universally-validated R1 §4.R1 «Granola-grade content rewrite» layer of D already requires).

**Caveat: receipt-chrome amplifies content-quality requirements.** Once chrome promises «every claim cites itself,» any tab that lacks a visible cite reads as broken-promise. Receipt-chrome adoption is contingent on completing the Granola-grade content rewrite. If content-lead can't deliver real-feeling cite-trails for all four demo tabs, receipt-chrome is premature.

## §5. Hero retire question (Q4)

CD proposes: drop L2 (insight feed) + L3 (broker pie) from hero, replace L1 with single «receipt» panel + 2 quiet footers.

**My ruling: KEEP-L1-RETIRE-L2-L3 — but don't replace L1 with a receipt panel; instead, evolve L1 in receipt-chrome direction.** Specific reasoning below.

### Does the 3-mockup stack carry positioning value or dilute it?

Positioning value of the current 3-mockup stack:
- **L1 (chat-surface ChatMockup):** carries the «chat-first» wedge that R2 §6.Opp-1 identifies as uncontested in direct fintech category. **High positioning value.** This is the differentiator.
- **L2 (insight feed):** demonstrates pattern-detection capability. **Medium positioning value.** But pattern-detection is also delivered in §S5 insights bullets and §S4 demo Tab «Notice.» Some redundancy.
- **L3 (broker pie):** visualizes multi-broker aggregation. **Low-to-medium positioning value at hero scope.** Aggregation is also delivered in §S2 proof-bar «1000+ brokers» + §S8 marquee. Substantial redundancy.

**The positioning load is concentrated in L1.** L2 and L3 are demonstration-of-capability layers — they prove Provedo can do the things, but they don't carry brand-territory equity that other sections don't already carry.

### Is single-receipt hero stronger or weaker on positioning equity?

**Stronger on receipt-equity. Weaker on chat-first wedge.** Trade-off:
- A single-receipt hero would be the most-distinctive landing in the audited fintech set on the receipt axis. Positioning equity gain: real and structural.
- A single-receipt hero would **drop the chat-surface visualization that anchors the chat-first wedge above the fold.** Chat-first is the strongest competitive differentiation finding in R2 (§6.Opp-1). Dropping it from the hero level pushes the wedge to §S4 demo tabs only. Cold visitors who don't scroll past hero never see the wedge.

**Net positioning-equity verdict:** the trade is roughly neutral on equity (gain receipt-territory, lose chat-territory) but **shifts the bet entirely off the validated wedge (chat-first uncontested in direct fintech, R2 §6.Opp-1 8/8 confidence) onto an unvalidated wedge (receipt-chrome unclaimed but never tested in fintech context, R1 + R2 inferred 5-7/8 confidence).** Pre-alpha, swapping a high-confidence wedge for a moderate-confidence wedge is positioning-equity-negative.

### Recommended action: KEEP-L1-RETIRE-L2-L3

Specifically:
1. **Keep L1 (ChatMockup)** as hero anchor. It carries the validated wedge.
2. **Retire L2 + L3 from hero.** They duplicate proof carried elsewhere on the page; their hero placement is decoration-of-density, not positioning-load.
3. **Evolve L1 in receipt-chrome direction.** Add a visible cite-line under the AI response in ChatMockup («Source: AAPL Q3 earnings 2025-10-31 · Schwab statement 2025-11-01»). Add a small typographic «Sources:» primitive under the response. This converts L1 from «chat-mockup» to «chat-mockup with receipt-chrome» — gaining receipt-territory equity without sacrificing chat-first wedge.
4. **Use the L2/L3 visual budget freed by retirement** for: (a) a quiet «Sources:» strip somewhere mid-page (R2 §6.Opp-5 «sources for every observation» strip — already in Option B spec, but D can adopt it), and (b) a tightened proof-bar with one less cell (drop the now-redundant duplicate broker count if marquee carries it).

This captures CD's structural intent (receipt-chrome as load-bearing visual primitive) without the wedge-sacrifice cost of a full hero retire.

## §6. Sequencing question (Q5)

CD proposes: 1-week PR with 1 big move (hero retire) + 6 small moves.

**My ruling: STAGED-2-SLICES.** Specifically:
- **Slice-LP4-D-prime-A (1 week):** ship the receipt-chrome layer on top of the already-shipped slice-LP3.2. Specifically: L2/L3 retirement from hero + ChatMockup cite-line addition + sources-strip mid-page (replacing or augmenting the broker marquee) + footer disclaimer Layer 1 receipt-typography pass. **6 small moves, all reversible at the chrome level, no hero copy change, no posture commitment.** Single PR.
- **Slice-LP4-D-prime-B (later, after either trigger evidence OR PO explicit go):** the deeper visual-system extension — sources-as-typographic-primitive across all sections, receipt-style demo-tab chrome, full Stripe-Press-meets-Linear-changelog typographic discipline. This is bigger; needs product-designer + frontend-engineer coordinated work; benefits from observing how A-slice lands.

**Why staged, not single-PR-all-in:**
1. **Reversibility asymmetry.** Slice-A is chrome-only — every change can be reverted by un-doing the typographic pass. Slice-B touches structural rhythm of the page and is harder to revert without a v3-rollback.
2. **Validation budget.** Slice-A's six small moves can be observed live (do visitors who arrive on the chrome-evolved page parse it correctly? do dwell-time/scroll-depth instruments show degradation?). Slice-B should be informed by whatever signal slice-A surfaces.
3. **Content-fidelity dependency.** Slice-B amplifies the load on demo-tab content fidelity (every tab must visibly cite). Granola-grade content rewrite is a content-lead deliverable that should land before slice-B chrome lands. Decoupling lets content-lead work run in parallel with slice-A frontend-engineer work.
4. **Pre-alpha-no-data discipline.** My prior verdict's load-bearing principle stands: at pre-alpha, optionality preservation is positioning-equity-correct. Single-PR-all-in collapses optionality across two layers (chrome + structural rhythm) simultaneously. Staging preserves the option to ship slice-A and stop there if it turns out to be sufficient.

**Disagreement with CD framing:** «1 big move + 6 small moves in 1 PR» reads as project-management velocity reasoning, not positioning-equity reasoning. The «atomic» framing assumes the changes need to land together to read as a coherent direction. They don't — slice-A is observably-coherent on its own (the chrome evolution), and slice-B can layer on once slice-A is digested.

## §8. Cross-cutting positioning-equity risks if CD direction adopted

### Risk #1 — Chrome-promise-content-mismatch

If receipt-chrome is shipped before Granola-grade demo content rewrite lands, the page promises citation-discipline and delivers placeholder-content. **Negative equity.** Mitigation: gate slice-A on content-lead delivery of cite-trail content for at minimum the hero ChatMockup + one demo tab. Do not ship sources-strip without source content.

### Risk #2 — Receipt-chrome reads as «old-school finance/legal stiffness» if executed wrong

The Stripe-Press / courtroom-exhibit reference cluster has a real failure mode: it can read as legal-document-stiff (anti-Everyman-warmth modifier in the locked archetype mix). The 04_BRAND.md voice fingerprint is **Magician + Sage primary · Everyman modifier** — Everyman warmth is a load-bearing modifier that receipt-chrome can crush if executed at full Stripe-Press intensity.

Mitigation: receipt-chrome must be executed at the **restrained-Sage end**, not the full-courtroom-exhibit end. Reference targeting: Linear changelog (more) + Stripe Press (less) + Anthropic warm-paper (more) + courtroom-exhibit (none). Brand-voice-curator pass on the chrome typography is required pre-ship.

### Risk #3 — Receipt-chrome can over-rotate Sage and subdue Magician

This is the same risk Option C carried (negation hero subdues Magician). Receipt-chrome is Sage-primary by construction (calm observation, evidence-discipline). The Magician archetype (pattern synthesis, foresight, the «I provide for / I foresee» etymology) needs surface area to breathe. If chrome is uniformly receipt-coded across the page, the Magician beat (the chat surface coming alive, the typing animation, the live demo) gets visually subdued.

Mitigation: keep the typing animation in L1 (ChatMockup). Keep the chart-animation work from `ca6f53d`. Receipt-chrome lives in **non-product-surface** chrome (sources-strip, footer, demo-tab cite-lines), not on the product-surface visualizations themselves. The product surfaces stay Magician-coded; the supporting chrome is Sage-coded. Both archetypes hold.

### Risk #4 — Receipt-chrome is Provedo-honest only as long as Provedo's product is actually receipt-honest

The chrome makes a structural promise about the product's epistemic discipline. The product MUST honor it. Specifically: every AI response in production must visibly cite, every dashboard insight must carry a source-link, every coach observation must trace to a specific dated event. If Provedo's eventual production product ships with un-cited responses (which happens in early AI products under shipping pressure), the chrome converts from positive equity to **brand-fraud signal** — landing-promised what product didn't deliver.

Mitigation: **route this specifically to tech-lead via right-hand.** Receipt-chrome on landing should be gated on tech-lead confirmation that the alpha product can ship with cite-discipline at chrome-promised level. If tech-lead says cite-discipline isn't reliably shippable in alpha, defer slice-A.

### Risk #5 — «Receipts» metaphor may read transactional/commerce-coded to some visitors

The word «receipt» in fintech-adjacent contexts has a specific commerce association (payment receipts, transaction receipts). Receipt-chrome on a portfolio-tracker landing could prime the visitor to expect a payments product or a transaction-reconciliation product — neither of which Provedo is. **Low-probability risk** but worth flagging.

Mitigation: use «sources» as the typographic primitive, not «receipts.» Sources is the verb-form Provedo's allowlist already carries («provides sources» is on the allowlist; «cites» is on the allowlist). The brief's framing of «receipts as thesis» is CD's vocabulary; the implementation should use Provedo's vocabulary. Internal-frame: receipt-chrome principle. External-copy: «Sources:» typography.
