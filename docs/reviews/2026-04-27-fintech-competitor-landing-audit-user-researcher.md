## §2. Per-competitor cards

### 2.1 Snowball Analytics — `snowball-analytics.com`

**Hero pitch.** «Simple and powerful portfolio tracker» / «All your investments in one place / Know your real performance / Automate dividend tracking» / CTA «Get started for free».

**30-second test.** ICP-clear: self-directed retail investor with multiple brokers + dividends. Product-clear: aggregation + dividend tracker + performance vs benchmark. Pricing not on hero (driven to /pricing). What it doesn't do: chat, AI, narrative — pure dashboard product.

**Voice register.** Technical, feature-listed, **information-only**. Three representative phrases: «Track thousands of stocks, ETFs and funds» · «Support automatic brokerage connections with over 1,000 brokers» · «See the future dividend payouts for the year ahead». Functional, dry, zero advice gradient.

**Lane A discipline.** **Cleanest in the set.** Explicit landing-level disclaimer: «not a registered investment advisor» / «for educational and entertainment purposes only, and do not constitute investment advice». No advice / recommendation / strategy language anywhere. Lane A score: **0/5** — pure information.

**Proof structure.** 4.8★ App Store (4,200 reviews) + 4.8★ Google Play (2,300 reviews). Benzinga 2023 review. Product Hunt badge. No testimonials. No customer logos. No press grid.

**Pricing transparency.** Buried in /pricing. Free CTA on hero. Roughly $5/mo per public benchmarks.

**«Why us» framing.** Implicit — «1,000 brokers» + «real performance» + «dividend tracking» as the trinity. No explicit differentiator paragraph.

**What feels weak.** Hero reads commodity. No editorial voice. No moment of «why this one over the other 12 dividend trackers». Page is honest but forgettable.

### 2.2 Copilot Money — `copilot.money`

**Hero pitch.** «Your money, beautifully organized.» / «All of your accounts, spending, and investments, automatically tracked.» / CTA «Get started».

**30-second test.** Aspirational + design-led. ICP: design-conscious millennial managing accounts + spending + investments. Product-clear-ish: personal finance manager with AI categorization, NOT a pure portfolio tracker. Investments mentioned but secondary to spending/budgeting.

**Voice register.** Aspirational, design-led, friendly. Three representative phrases: «Your money, beautifully organized» · «AI learns your spending patterns» · «No need to jump between five different apps to see your net worth». Verb register: tracks / learns / spots / shows.

**Lane A discipline.** **Drift detected.** «Get personalized recommendations» appears in product copy. Apple's own «Editor's Choice» framing. Term «recommendations» is the exact 5-item EN guardrail violation pattern Provedo bans. Score: **2/5** — explicit advice-adjacent term, but applied to subscriptions/spending («spot subscriptions») rather than investment moves; advisor-drift exists but is contained to non-investment domain.

**Proof structure.** Apple Design Awards Finalist · MKBHD endorsement (named) · 10+ App Store testimonial blocks · Editor's Choice mention.

**Pricing transparency.** **On landing.** $7.92/mo annual or $13/mo monthly. Free trial advertised.

**«Why us» framing.** Design + AI categorization speed + Apple-design pedigree. Differentiates by aesthetic + platform-trust signal.

**What feels weak.** Investment tracking is third-fiddle to spending + budgets. Lane A drift on «recommendations» is the kind of casual SaaS phrasing Provedo's guardrails were built to prevent. Aspirational hero («beautifully organized») is high-budget but content-light — passes 30-sec test on aesthetic, not on JTBD specificity.

### 2.3 Monarch Money — `monarch.com`

**Hero pitch.** «The modern way to manage money» / «Budget, track your progress, and plan for the future—all in one app.» / CTA «Unlock better money habits».

**30-second test.** ICP-broad: anyone managing personal finances, especially couples (collaborative-money framing repeats). Product: budgeting-first, with investments as a sub-feature. Mint-replacement positioning.

**Voice register.** Friendly-aspirational, behavior-coded («habits»), collaborative. Three representative phrases: «modern way to manage money» · «track, budget, collaborate, and set goals specific to you» · «Always know where your money is and where it's going». Verb register: track / budget / plan / collaborate.

**Lane A discipline.** **Surprisingly clean.** No explicit advice/recommendation language despite being a $100/yr premium product. «Plan for the future» borders on planning-claim register but stays observational. Score: **1/5** — language brushes against goal-planning paternalism via «better money habits» CTA but stays in self-help/behavior-change framing rather than investment-recommendation framing.

**Proof structure.** WSJ «best budgeting app overall for 2024» · ZDNET «My top pick» · Forbes / USA Today / Elite Daily / The Cut press grid · 3 App Store testimonial blocks. Press-heavy.

**Pricing transparency.** Hero advertises «free 7-day trial» + 30% OFF code WELCOME. Actual price ($99.99/yr) on /pricing.

**«Why us» framing.** Mint-replacement (implicit) + couples/collaboration + reliability of bank connections. Moat: «connections to institutions way more reliable» (testimonial).

**What feels weak.** Investment tracking is implicit, not anchor — competitors at this price tier *should* claim investment-tracking depth more loudly. CTA «Unlock better money habits» is the most paternalistic line in the set («unlock» implies you're locked out of being good with money) — Lane A clean but voice-register questionable.

### 2.4 Getquin — `getquin.com`

**Hero pitch.** «Your entire wealth. One platform.» / «Track your net worth, plan for retirement, and optimize your wealth with powerful analytics, AI tools, and expert advice—all in one platform.» / CTA «Start now».

**30-second test.** ICP: affluent EU retail investor, multi-account holder, retirement-planning. Product: portfolio + retirement + AI agent + community. Comprehensive but cluttered claim — «expert advice» is the load-bearing scope-claim.

**Voice register.** Aspirational, affluent-coded, advice-y. Three representative phrases: «Your entire wealth» · «optimize your wealth with...expert advice» · «Ask your AI financial agent anything». Verb register: optimize / advise / plan / ask.

**Lane A discipline.** **Worst drift in the set.** «Expert advice» on hero sub. «AI financial agent» as feature claim. «Optimize your wealth» as outcome claim. «Risk ratings» as analytic output. Three guardrail violations co-occurring on the hero alone. Score: **4/5** — explicit advice claim + AI-agent advisor positioning + outcome optimization. Only Range (in prior audit) and possibly Bobby/Magnifi exceed this.

**Proof structure.** «+€20B Tracked» · «+500K Users» · «10K+ 5-star ratings» · 3 App Store testimonials · «Featured on:» logo grid (logos not visible in markdown extraction).

**Pricing transparency.** Not on landing. Sign-up-gated. Public benchmark: €7.50/mo Plus tier.

**«Why us» framing.** EU-native + community + AI-agent + comprehensive (track + plan + optimize). Multi-claim, low-anchor.

**What feels weak.** Lane A drift is the headline finding. «Expert advice» on hero will trigger any retail investor with regulatory awareness — and Provedo's ICP A (multi-broker millennial, productivity-native cohort) is exactly that audience. Getquin's own legal position likely has BaFin disclaimers buried in footnotes, but the landing voice contradicts the disclaim. JTBD-overload — page tries to anchor 4 jobs at once.

### 2.5 Kubera — `kubera.com`

**Hero pitch.** «The Balance Sheet for those who manage their own wealth» / «$250 a year. Whether you have $250K or $2.5 billion.» / CTA «Try for 14 days».

**30-second test.** ICP-narrow + crystal-clear: HNW self-directed individual, 7-figure+ net worth, multi-asset (RE / crypto / private equity / DeFi / collectibles). Product: net-worth aggregation, NOT performance tracking, NOT advisor. Premium-priced single-tier product.

**Voice register.** Premium, self-aware, anti-advisor. Three representative phrases: «I don't want budgeting. I don't want an advisor pitch» · «Skip the middleman» · «$250 a year. Whether you have $250K or $2.5 billion». Verb register: aggregate / unify / track / skip-the-middleman.

**Lane A discipline.** **Lane A by negation — second-cleanest in the set after Snowball.** Explicit anti-advisor positioning is the entire pitch. «Skip the middleman» = anti-1%-AUM. Zero advice / recommendation / strategy language. Score: **0/5** — Lane A as positioning copy, even cleaner than Snowball because Kubera *makes the negation a feature*. Provedo's S3 problem-negation section pattern is essentially the Kubera move applied to AI-chat domain.

**Proof structure.** **Strongest social proof in the set.** Named founder testimonials (Eoghan McCabe — Intercom, Austin Rief — Morning Brew, Kevin Rose — True Ventures, Keith Poniewaz — IAS). $47.34B AUM tracked. No press grid; testimonial-led.

**Pricing transparency.** **On hero, in tagline.** $250/year. Plus Kubera Black ($2,500/yr) + White Label ($300+/mo) tiers transparent.

**«Why us» framing.** Anti-advisor + flat-fee + multi-asset + dead-man's-switch (generational wealth transfer). Sharpest differentiation in set.

**What feels weak.** Almost nothing — Kubera is the strongest pitch in the 7. Mild critique: hero CTA «Try for 14 days» is generic; could match the rest of the page's confidence with a sharper line. ICP narrowness is a feature, not a flaw, but only addressable by HNW visitors — Provedo cannot import the price-anchor framing.

**Direct study target for Provedo.** Kubera's negation + named-testimonial + price-on-hero + ICP-specificity is the closest analog of what Provedo's v3.1 is reaching for.

### 2.6 Sharesight — `sharesight.com`

**Hero pitch.** «Be the smarter investor» / «Powerful portfolio tracking software that lets you check your investments in one place with award-winning performance, dividend tracking and tax reporting.» / CTA «Sign up for free».

**30-second test.** ICP: retail investor with global holdings, tax-conscious, dividend-focused. Product: tracker + dividend + tax-report. Australia/NZ legacy, expanding global. ICP not super-clear (10-holding-tier suggests very-low-end accessible).

**Voice register.** Confident-functional, slightly self-improvement coded («smarter investor»), feature-led. Three representative phrases: «Be the smarter investor» · «check your investments in one place» · «award-winning performance, dividend tracking and tax reporting». Verb register: check / track / report.

**Lane A discipline.** **Near-cleanest, with a wrinkle.** Hero «Be the smarter investor» implies a normative-improvement frame — slight Lane A risk because «smarter» implies a measurable improvement Sharesight contributes to. No advice/recommendation/strategy language elsewhere. Score: **1/5** — the «smarter investor» framing is the only blemish; rest is information-only.

**Proof structure.** 500K investors · 700K assets supported · Financial Review / Investopedia / IBD press · Trustpilot link · 200 broker integrations including Robinhood, Schwab, IBKR.

**Pricing transparency.** **On landing.** Free (10 holdings) · $7/mo Starter · $18/mo Standard · $23.25/mo Premium. Most price-transparent in the set.

**«Why us» framing.** Tax-reporting depth + global broker coverage + dividend specialization. Accountant-grade angle Provedo cannot match (and shouldn't try).

Visual is feature-grid commodity. ICP not anchored — page works for $5K-portfolio newcomer AND $500K-portfolio active investor, which means it doesn't really land for either.

### 2.7 Wealthfolio — `wealthfolio.app`

**Hero pitch.** «Grow Wealth. Keep Control.» / «A beautiful, private and open-source investment tracker that runs locally on all your devices.» / CTA «Get Wealthfolio».

**30-second test.** ICP-clear: privacy-first / open-source-curious / local-first investor. Product: local-runtime portfolio tracker, optional cloud sync. The cleanest «hook» in the set after Kubera.

**Voice register.** Technical, principled, slightly developer-coded. Three representative phrases: «Grow Wealth. Keep Control.» · «runs entirely on your devices» · «100% Open Source: Inspect, fork, run your way». Verb register: grow / control / inspect / fork / sync.

**Lane A discipline.** **Tied with Snowball / Kubera at cleanest.** No advice / recommendation / strategy language. «Ask questions about your portfolio and get AI-powered insights» is the closest-to-Provedo phrasing in the entire 7 — and stays observation-only («insights», not «recommendations»). Score: **0/5** — pure information, zero advisor drift.

**Proof structure.** 6,260 GitHub stars · Hacker News feature · Product Hunt feature · Trendshift badge. Developer-credential proof, no testimonials, no press.

**Pricing transparency.** Mostly absent. «Connect is an optional subscription» without price. App itself implied free (open-source).

**«Why us» framing.** Privacy + local-first + open-source + AI insights. Differentiates by trust-architecture (your data stays on your device) rather than feature depth.

**What feels weak.** Pricing opacity is a wedge against converting to Plus tier. Open-source/local-first ICP is small (technical investors only). «Grow Wealth. Keep Control.» is good but the «Grow Wealth» half is performance-promise-adjacent — Lane A passes only because it's framed as user-side outcome (you grow, the app keeps your control), not product capability.

**Direct study target for Provedo's chat tab claim.** Wealthfolio's «Ask questions about your portfolio and get AI-powered insights» is **the only direct competitor in the set making the chat-first claim**. Provedo's positioning hypothesis (chat-first preference over dashboard-first) is currently uncontested by all 7 except Wealthfolio's tertiary mention. **Major finding: Provedo's chat-first wedge is real.**

## §6. Differentiation opportunities for Provedo (3-5)

### Opp #1 — Chat-first hero (uncontested in direct category)

**Evidence.** 0 of 7 direct portfolio-tracker peers leads with chat-first claim on the hero. Wealthfolio mentions AI insights tertiary. Getquin claims AI-financial-agent but bundled into a 4-job hero overload. **Provedo's hero «Provedo will lead you through your portfolio» + «Ask Provedo» CTA is unclaimed wedge in the direct-competitor set.**

**Recommendation.** Hold. Don't dilute hero with multi-job overload. Resist temptation to add «track + plan + insights» style multi-claim — that's the Getquin trap. The single-claim hero is the differentiation.

### Opp #2 — Lane A as positioning copy, not just disclaimer

**Evidence.** Snowball relegates Lane A to footer disclaimer. Kubera makes anti-advisor a *feature* («Skip the middleman»). Provedo's S3 problem-negation section is Kubera's move applied to AI-chat — but Kubera goes further: «Skip the middleman» is on hero CTA-area, not buried in S3.

**Recommendation.** Test promoting Lane A negation higher. Either (a) hero sub-line variant: «Notice what you'd miss across all your brokers — without an advisor pitch.» Or (b) move S3 problem-negation above S2 numeric proof (currently the order is hero → proof → negation; consider hero → negation → proof).

### Opp #3 — Pattern-detection as hero claim, not just demo tab

**Evidence.** «Pattern recognition» / «patterns you would have missed» / «patterns from your past trades» — none of the 7 anchors this on hero. Snowball + Sharesight + Wealthfolio all do tracking; nobody does retrospective pattern surfacing as primary hero claim.

**Recommendation.** Consider a hero variant testing pattern claim explicitly: «Provedo notices what you'd miss across all your brokers — patterns, drawdowns, dividends.» (currently sub-hero says «Notice what you'd miss» without naming the pattern artefacts). Bringing the artefact-list into sub-hero (patterns / drawdowns / dividends) anchors uncontested JTBD content above the fold.

### Opp #4 — «No advice» as feature, not compliance

**Evidence.** Provedo S3 has «Provedo is not a robo-advisor / not a brokerage / will not tell you what to buy». Kubera has «I don't want an advisor pitch». **Provedo can go further than Kubera by naming the *positive* outcome of no-advice: full ownership of decision-making.** None of the 7 competitors frames «no advice» as user-empowerment.

**Recommendation.** Add a single line near S3 closing: «You stay in charge. Provedo just shows you everything.» — or test in proof bar cell #4 («Lane A — information, not advice») with a more user-coded variant: «Every decision stays yours».

### Opp #5 — Source-cited observations as trust anchor

**Evidence.** 0 of 7 promises «every observation cited». Snowball, Sharesight, Wealthfolio show data; none promises traceability. Provedo's Tab 1-4 demos all include «sources cited inline» — but this is buried as parenthetical, not anchored as differentiator.

**Recommendation.** Promote «every observation cited» to numeric proof bar (already in v2 cell #2 «Every answer cites its source») — confirm visual prominence. Consider adding a single line after S3: «With sources for every observation. Always.» — Sage-trust-anchor that no peer carries.

## §7. Patterns Provedo should adopt (3-5)

### Adopt #1 — Price-on-hero (Kubera + Sharesight pattern)

**Evidence.** Kubera puts $250/yr in the hero sub-headline itself («$250 a year. Whether you have $250K or $2.5 billion.»). Sharesight breaks pricing into landing tiers visible on /pricing without sign-up. Monarch advertises «free 7-day trial» on hero. **Snowball, Getquin, Copilot, Wealthfolio bury or hide pricing.**

**Provedo v3.1 status.** Plus tier price ($X placeholder in S10 Q4 FAQ). Free tier framed as «No card. 50 chat messages a month, free always.» — strong, but Plus price absent.

**Recommendation.** When Plus price locked ($9/mo per finance), put it in S2 numeric proof bar as cell #5 or in S9 pre-footer CTA small-print. Not buried in FAQ. Kubera's tagline-pricing is too aggressive for Provedo's Sage register, but proof-bar price is in-character.

### Adopt #2 — Broker-coverage logos above the fold (4/7 pattern)

**Evidence.** Snowball mentions «1,000 brokers» on hero. Sharesight mentions «200 integrations». Getquin «connect all your accounts». Wealthfolio «all your brokers and banks in one view». **Provedo's broker marquee is currently in S8 (8th section).**

**Provedo v3.1 status.** S2 cell #1 has «1000+ brokers and exchanges» numeric (good). Marquee with logos is at S8.

**Recommendation.** Test promoting broker marquee earlier — between S2 (proof bar) and S3 (problem-negation). Or: reduce S8 marquee to a thin logo strip immediately under S2 proof bar. The «1000+» number is doing work in proof bar but isn't *visualized*. 4/7 competitors lead with broker-trust signal closer to the fold than Provedo.

### Adopt #3 — Named testimonials with title-credentialed authors (Kubera pattern)

**Evidence.** Kubera testimonials = Eoghan McCabe (Intercom), Austin Rief (Morning Brew), Kevin Rose (True Ventures), Keith Poniewaz (IAS). Names + companies. Strong-tier (prior audit) Stripe / Cursor / Linear all do this. Snowball / Wealthfolio / Copilot use generic App Store quotes; weaker.

**Provedo v3.1 status.** S7 builder testimonials with «Roman M., builder at Provedo» framing. Honest but pre-alpha.

**Recommendation.** Once alpha launches, prioritize getting 3 named testimonials from credentialed alpha-testers (productivity-tool-adjacent founders, fintech-Twitter accounts, multi-broker investor podcasters). Even single high-credibility name (Notion / Linear / Cursor user with public profile) outweighs 10 anonymous quotes. Until then, builder framing is correct.

### Adopt #4 — JTBD specificity in ICP language (Kubera pattern)

**Evidence.** Kubera: «for those who manage their own wealth». Single sentence, audience nailed. Sharesight tries the same but vaguer («smarter investor»). Provedo's hero «for investors who hold across more than one broker» (from prior audit C5 spec) is exactly this pattern.

**Provedo v3.1 status.** Audience-specificity NOT currently in v2 hero. Was a candidate in prior audit (C5: «For investors holding across more than one broker» / «Built for portfolios that don't fit in one app» / «For people who own things in seven places»). v2 doesn't ship any of these.

**Recommendation.** Add audience-specificity line as proof-bar adjacent or as hero whisper. Recommend: **«For investors who hold across more than one broker.»** — single sentence, names ICP A directly, anchors the multi-broker JTBD that Snowball / Wealthfolio / Getquin all claim but none anchor as an audience definition.
