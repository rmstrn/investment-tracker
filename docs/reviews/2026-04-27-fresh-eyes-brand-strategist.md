# Fresh-eyes positioning audit — Provedo landing

**Reviewer:** Outside brand strategist (no prior project context).
**Date:** 2026-04-27
**Source read:** Live preview returned 401, fell back to React components in `apps/web/src/app/(marketing)/_components/` — full hero, S2-S10 sections, header, footer. No `/docs` consulted.
**Read time:** ~30 seconds equivalent.

---

## 1. The 30-second product description

> A read-only chat assistant that pulls all your brokerage and crypto accounts into one place and answers «what's happening in my portfolio» questions with cited sources — without telling you what to buy or moving any money.

Closest mental model for a colleague: «Perplexity for your portfolio, with broker aggregation built in». Not an advisor. Not a trader. A reader and a noticer.

---

## 2. Positioning category the page claims

The page is staking out a category the industry doesn't have a clean name for yet — closest framing: **portfolio intelligence layer**, or in the page's own register, a **multi-broker portfolio reader with cited observation**.

Three category-defining moves are visible:

1. **Negation card** explicitly rejects three adjacent categories: robo-advisor, brokerage, advice. That is real positioning work — most fintech-AI startups will not say what they're not.
2. **«Holds» / «notices» / «source-keeper»** verbs replace the standard «track / analyze / optimize» fintech vocabulary. The product wants to be felt as custodial of context, not custodial of money.
3. **«Sources for every answer»** is treated as the hero proof point in the bento — a deliberate parallel to Perplexity's citation-first AI search, transposed into finance.

The bet: **«AI portfolio chat with citations»** is a defensible new category once enough people have an AI-fatigue allergy to confidently-wrong fintech bots.

---

## 3. ICP read from the page alone

The page reads to a very specific person without naming them:

- **Multi-account self-directed investor.** The broker marquee (IBKR, Schwab, Fidelity, Coinbase, Binance, IBKR + Schwab in the demo) plus the editorial line «your portfolio lives in seven places» locks this in. They have ≥ 3 accounts across stocks + crypto.
- **Reasonably sophisticated.** The bento answer says «tech is 58% of your equity exposure — about 2x the sector's weight in S&P 500 (~28%)». Anyone who needs that explained is not the target.
- **Allergic to robo-advice and bro-trader content.** The disclaim register («no advice», «every decision stays yours», «read-only API») is a reassurance ritual aimed at people who have been burned by or are skeptical of TikTok-finance, Robinhood gamification, and Wealthfront-style auto-rebalancing.
- **Time-poor.** «5 min a week» is positioned as the whole habit. They want clarity, not a hobby.

Demographic guess: 30-45, urban, $100k-$1M portfolio across 3-7 accounts, tech-adjacent profession, English-language UI.

What's NOT visible: the page does not commit to «retail», «pro», «family office», or «crypto-native». That ambiguity is mostly a feature here.

---

## 4. The «only» — what makes this feel like the only X

> **The only AI portfolio chat that will read every broker you own and refuses to tell you what to buy.**

That sentence is the convergence point of three claims the page makes simultaneously:

- Multi-broker aggregation (the marquee + «hundreds of brokers»)
- Chat-first interface with cited answers (the hero conversation + «sources for every answer» bento)
- Hard refusal of advice as a brand stance, not a regulatory footnote (the negation card)

No single competitor in the 2026 fintech-AI landscape does all three with conviction. Magnifi gives recommendations. Kubera aggregates but is dashboard-shaped, not chat-shaped. Sharesight does tax-and-performance reports. Wealthfolio is open-source local-only. Range and Mercury aren't even in the ICP. **The «only» is real and defensible — for now.**

---

## 5. Top 3 strategic moves working

1. **The negation card is doing 60% of the positioning work.** «What Provedo is not / is» reads as confidence, not defensiveness. Most fintech-AI startups will not publicly say «we are not advice» because their growth team thinks it kills conversion. Provedo turns it into a wedge. This is the most distinctive move on the page.

2. **«Sources for every answer» as the hero bento cell.** The teal-tinted treatment makes this the visual anchor of the proof bar. In a market where every AI fintech is fighting trust deficit (hallucination fear, Robinhood PTSD, ChatGPT-told-me-to-buy-NVDA jokes), this is the right thing to anchor on. It also borrows credibility from the Perplexity playbook, which the target ICP knows.

3. **Tone restraint on the closer: «You hold the assets. Provedo holds the context.»** This is the only line on the page that approaches taglineable. It's good. It says ownership of decisions stays with the user, while the product handles cognitive load. It also rhymes the brand name etymologically without being precious about it.

---

## 6. Top 3 strategic gaps — claims left on the table

1. **No moment of «watch this work».** The S4 bento shows two static-looking demo answers. There's no live demo, no «paste your CSV», no «try it on a sample portfolio», no animated typing of a fresh question. For a chat-first product, this is the central conversion gap. Magnifi, Range, and Mercury all have interactive demos on landing. The page says «picture-first hero» but the picture is a frozen receipt.

2. **The privacy / read-only story is buried.** «Read-only API connections, no trading credentials, no money movement» sits in FAQ Q5. For an ICP that is allergic to giving credentials to anything new, this should be a hero-adjacent trust signal — proof bar cell, atmosphere callout, or sub-headline. Right now the page asks a multi-account investor to mentally connect 7 brokers without addressing the fear until the FAQ.

3. **«Patterns in your past trades» is the most differentiated claim and the page barely uses it.** The hero sub-line says «notice what you'd miss across all your brokers». The OG description mentions «patterns in your past trades». No section actually shows this. Behavioral pattern recognition on someone's trade history is the genuinely novel capability — Kubera/Sharesight/Getquin can't do it, even Magnifi doesn't try. Leaving it as an OG-tag throwaway is leaving the strongest claim on the table.

Two smaller leaks worth flagging:

- **«Pre-alpha» surfaced in FAQ Q6 plus the page has no testimonials / no logos / no «trusted by» strip.** The page is honest about being early. That's brave but it caps conversion. Even one beta user quote with a face would shift the trust math.
- **«Hundreds of brokers» is hedged copy.** Either it's «1000+» (a number that travels) or it's «every major broker, named» (a list that builds confidence). «Hundreds» is the worst of both — vague enough to feel padded, specific enough to invite verification.

---

## 7. Differentiation score vs 2026 fintech-AI landscape

**7 / 10.**

- The category claim is genuine and the «only» is real — that's worth 5 points by itself.
- The negation/positioning move and source-citation discipline add 2 more points; both are differentiated and few competitors will copy them quickly because their commercial model depends on advice or commerce.
- Held back by: no interactive demo (−1), buried trust story (−1), and the strongest behavioral-pattern claim is unused on-page (−1).

If the page added a working demo and surfaced the read-only / patterns claims at hero level, this gets to a 9. If they also cracked a single sticky tagline and shipped one named beta-user quote, it's a 10.

---

## 8. Closing read

This is one of the few fintech-AI landings I've seen this year that has a real point of view. It knows what it is, knows what it isn't, and is willing to say so out loud. The voice is the most mature thing on the page; the proof apparatus is the second; the conversion engineering is the weakest. The fix isn't a rebrand — it's a demo and two trust moves. The strategy is correct. The page just hasn't fully shipped the strategy yet.
