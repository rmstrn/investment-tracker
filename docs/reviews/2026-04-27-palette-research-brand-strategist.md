# Palette Research 2026 — Brand-Strategist

**Date:** 2026-04-27
**Author:** brand-strategist (isolated context, dispatched by Right-Hand)
**Scope:** Brand-fit palette recommendation for Provedo. Visual direction (tactile/soft 3D V4) is locked; only palette is open.

**Recommended palette:**
- BG: **#F4F1EA** (warm cream, paper-coded)
- Card: **#FAF7F0** (lifted cream, single soft shadow on top of BG)
- Ink: **#1A1A1A** (deep ink, not pure black)
- Accent (primary): **#3F5D4A** (deep sage / forest)
- Accent (secondary, sparing): **#B8704D** (oxidized terracotta — for citation chips, drift pen-marks, single-moment warmth)

**Confidence:** high

This is option C (warm-white + olive) tightened. C beats A on warmth and B on archetype-fit by a clear margin. The deeper sage replaces C's olive #4A6741 because olive at that lightness reads dated; #3F5D4A reads forested, considered, current.

## Section 1 — Magician+Sage palette signature (5-7 brands)

The archetype mix is the main filter. Magician = quietly inventive (paper, ink, a pen — not glass and chrome). Sage = knows, does not preach (editorial restraint, hierarchy through type, not through hue). Both axes pull AWAY from saturation and TOWARD restraint. The Everyman modifier is the only axis that wants warmth — and warmth in palette terms means cream not white, sage not money-green, ink not jet.

The brands that carry Magician+Sage primary (as opposed to Magician+Hero like Stripe, or Sage+Caregiver like Vanguard) all sit in a narrow palette band:

| Brand | Archetype read | Palette signature | What it tells us |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Stripe Press** | Magician+Sage (publishing arm specifically, not Stripe product) | Warm cream paper #F6F1E8 / deep ink #1B1B1B / a single ink-tinted accent per book (oxblood, navy, forest) | Restraint + paper foundation + accent earned per-context, not standing accent |
| **Anthropic** | Magician+Sage | Off-white #FAFAF7 / charcoal #1A1A1A / a single warm coral accent #D97757 used SPARINGLY | Even AI's most Sage-coded brand uses a coral, not a green. Not a defensive choice — an offensive one (warmth as differentiator from cold-AI competitors) |
| **Are.na** | Magician+Sage (research-platform Magician) | Cream #F8F6EE / black ink / muted indigo for links | Editorial-flat with warmth. Zero saturation in the chrome |
| **Granola** | Magician+Sage (note-taking AI) | Warm white #F6F2EA / forest green accent #2D5A3D / soft card depth | Closest archetype peer to Provedo. Forest green, not money-green. Tactile-as-paper, not tactile-as-plastic |
| **The Browser Company / Arc** | Magician+Hero (more performative than Provedo) | Off-white / multi-gradient brand color wash | Useful counter-example: more Hero, more saturation. Provedo is one notch quieter than Arc |
| **Craig Mod's properties (Roden / Special Projects)** | Sage-pure (publishing/walking) | Cream paper #F4EFE5 / ink / occasional muted red | Voice reference for Provedo. Palette extends voice — paper, ink, one warm accent reserved for emphasis |
| **The Economist** | Sage-Ruler hybrid | White / red wordmark / charcoal | Counter-example with Ruler authority. Provedo is less institutional, so red flag is wrong; we go forest |
| **Patagonia (product pages)** | Sage+Caregiver | Warm white / forest green / earth tones | Pure proof-point that forest green carries «considered, restrained, trust» — same direction we're going |

**Pattern.** Six of the seven Magician+Sage references use cream-and-ink as base (not pure white). Five of seven use a muted-warm-or-forest accent (not money-green). The closest peer (Granola) and the closest voice reference (Patagonia, Craig Mod) both use forest-green-on-cream. **The «right» palette for this archetype mix is muted and restrained**, not bright and clean. Bright-and-clean palette (option B) belongs to Magician+Hero brands (Vercel, Linear, Brex) — a different archetype mix entirely.

## Section 2 — Competitor territory map

Mapping who owns what palette territory in the categories Provedo touches (finance + AI):

**Money-green #00875A territory (option B's accent):**
- Stripe (subtle, primarily on charts and CTAs, never as ambient brand color — Stripe brand is closer to indigo/purple)
- Brex (bright money-green on white, consumer-fintech-coded)
- Mercury (sage-green primary now; was money-green pre-2022 rebrand — they moved AWAY from money-green deliberately)
- Robinhood (bright trading-green, gamified — anti-positioning for Provedo)
- Cash App (bright teal-green, consumer toy)
- Wealthfront (muted green but with corporate-blue — confused brand)
- Plaid (deep money-green — but they're infra, not consumer)

Reading: money-green at consumer-facing brightness (#00875A and brighter) is colonized by trading apps and consumer-fintech. **Provedo moving into bright money-green = wedge ICP filtering OUT, not in.** Mercury's deliberate move from money-green TO sage in their 2022 rebrand is the most useful single signal — they had the same problem (Lane A trust, professional self-direction) and solved it by going muter.

**Sage / forest territory (option A and C accent):**
- Granola (forest green, AI notes — closest archetype peer)
- Mercury (sage primary post-2022 rebrand — direct fintech Lane-A peer doing exactly this move)
- Patagonia (forest, but different category — useful voice/values resonance, no fintech competition)
- Origin (sage with cream — multi-topic SEC advisor; semi-direct competitor on Lane B/C, NOT Lane A)
- Whoop (deep forest accent — wellness, not fintech)
- Linear (charcoal-with-purple, more recently sage in 2024 marketing pages — drifting toward our territory)

Reading: sage/forest in finance is **lightly populated** — Mercury and Origin are the only two consumer-finance brands here. Both are positioned as «considered alternatives» to bright-fintech. **This is defensible territory for Provedo, not crowded.** The fact that Mercury moved INTO it, not out of it, signals the trajectory of serious-consumer-fintech is toward muted greens, not away.

**Pure-white #FFFFFF territory (option B base):**
- Vercel, Linear, Anthropic (Anthropic is off-white, not pure), Notion product pages
- In finance: Mercury product UI (white-ish), Stripe Dashboard, Robinhood (with green chiclets)
- AI tooling: dominant — Vercel/Linear/Anthropic occupy this almost entirely

Reading: pure-white-and-charcoal is the **standard AI-tooling palette** for 2024-2026. Going there puts Provedo visually adjacent to Vercel-Linear-Anthropic, which is *correct for «AI tool» framing* but *wrong for «portfolio answer engine sitting between Perplexity and Bloomberg»* — because it makes us look like another developer-tool startup instead of a serious finance product. Pure-white also weakens the «paper» voice metaphor since paper isn't pure white in the world the voice references describe.

**Warm cream #F9F8F4 / #F4F1EA territory (option A and C base):**
- Are.na, Browser Co, Stripe Press, Anthropic (off-white #FAFAF7), Granola, Craig Mod publications, Patagonia
- In finance specifically: **near-empty.** Mercury uses near-white not cream; Origin uses cream-leaning but mixed with bright accents.

Reading: **warm cream in fintech is genuinely under-occupied territory.** This is the single strongest claim Provedo can make on palette: «the fintech that looks like a paper page, not a screen». Are.na, Browser, Granola, Stripe Press all cluster in this zone but none are competitors. Stepping into it gets us **archetype-amplification + competitor-territory ownership in one move**.

**Where Provedo can OWN a corner without me-too read:**
> **Warm cream paper + deep sage accent + ink + sparing oxidized terracotta for moments.**

This combination is unoccupied in fintech. It is occupied in editorial publishing (Stripe Press), AI knowledge-work (Granola), and outdoor/values-driven retail (Patagonia) — all *adjacent-but-non-competing* categories that lend trust transfer rather than create competitive overlap. This is a defensive moat the Vercel-stack option B can't claim.

## Section 3 — Voice-palette composition

The voice doc is unusually explicit about its references: **Patagonia + Craig Mod + Wirecutter + Economist + McPhee.** All five are paper-coded. Three out of five (Patagonia, Mod, McPhee) are explicitly walks-and-notices, slow-observed, declarative. The Economist gives sentence rhythm; Wirecutter gives non-selling stance.

Palette composition test for each voice reference:

- **Patagonia copy on warm cream + sage accent:** native fit. The actual Patagonia website does this exact thing.
- **Craig Mod's prose on warm cream + ink:** native fit. Craig Mod's actual newsletter design IS this palette (cream / ink / occasional muted red for emphasis).
- **Wirecutter review register on warm cream:** strong fit. Wirecutter's actual product is white-with-yellow, but the review *register* (long-form considered review prose) reads like a magazine page — cream-paper-coded.
- **Economist sentence rhythm on cream:** clean fit. The Economist is white-paper-coded specifically; cream is one warmth-step softer in the same family.
- **McPhee's noun-verb-period sentences on cream:** native fit. McPhee is published in the New Yorker, which is *literally cream paper*.

Now the same test on **option B's pure-white + money-green:**

- **Patagonia copy on pure white + #00875A:** dissonant. Patagonia would never use bright trading-green; Patagonia uses oxidized forest. The copy register and the palette register fight each other.
- **Craig Mod's walks-and-notices prose on Vercel-grade clean white:** dissonant. Mod's palette is paper-warm, never tech-bright.
- **Wirecutter's «I tested this» voice on a Brex-coded surface:** dissonant. Brex is finance-confident; Wirecutter is consumer-considered. Different registers.

Money-green primary CTA composes with «observant, composed» voice as a **direct contradiction**. «Observant» is the verb of someone holding still, watching. Money-green is the color of a stock ticker tick — the inverse stance. The voice says «notice»; money-green says «trade now». You can use money-green in this brand only if you accept that the surface and the prose will tell the user two different things at the same time.

**Sage accent vs money-green accent — which carries «calm intelligence» vs «pop alert»?**

- Sage **#3F5D4A** carries calm intelligence. It reads as forest, library, considered. It is the visual equivalent of «notice».
- Money-green **#00875A** carries pop alert. It reads as ticker, gain-celebration, urgency. It is the visual equivalent of «buy now».

For a brand whose hero-line verb is *notice* and whose locked accent stance is «information not advice» — sage is not just preferred, it is *demanded by the voice*. Money-green is the wrong instrument.

**Olive (option C original #4A6741) — confidently restrained or just dated?**

Olive at #4A6741 is borderline. It reads as competently restrained when paired with cream and good type (the Patagonia / Browser Co band), but it skews toward «mid-2010s organic-food packaging» without disciplined typography around it. The fix is to push the green slightly cooler and slightly deeper — **#3F5D4A** is forest-with-blue undertone, which reads contemporary (Whoop, Granola, Mercury 2024) rather than dated (Whole Foods 2014). At equal saturation, the bluer sage carries Sage-archetype intellectualism more cleanly than the yellower olive carries it. Same family, sharper read.

## Section 4 — Lane A trust signaling

Lane A makes color choice carry trust work the legal copy is forbidden from doing. The information-not-advice promise has to be visible in the palette.

**What color signals «I'm not a Robinhood-like trading toy»?**
- *Bright money-green*: the opposite signal. Robinhood IS bright money-green. Cash App teal-green and Acorns saturated-green are the same family. Walking into this palette = walking into the room as the new trading toy.
- *Sage / forest*: away from the toy register entirely. Mercury chose this exact palette to signal «we are the considered alternative». Provedo's Lane A position is the same problem with the same answer.
- *Pure white + charcoal*: neutral-but-empty. Reads as Vercel-stack startup, not as financial product. Doesn't fail the trust test, but doesn't pass it either — it dodges the question.

**What color signals «I won't tell you what to do, I'll show you what's there»?**
- *Restrained, paper-anchored, low-saturation palette*: yes. The Bloomberg Terminal proves this at the dense-professional end (flat / monospace / near-zero saturation); Stripe Press proves it at the editorial end (cream / ink / tiny accent). Both say «here is information, you decide».
- *High-saturation primary CTA*: actively contradicts «I'll show you what's there», because high-saturation buttons read as «click here NOW» — directional pressure the brand explicitly rejects.

**Does heavy money-green on hero CTA read as «trade now!!!» or «click here calmly»?**

It reads as «trade now». Specifically: a primary CTA at #00875A, full-width button, sitting under a hero that says «Provedo will lead you through your portfolio» creates the exact register mismatch the voice doc warns against. The button's color tells the user «time-pressured financial decision»; the prose around it tells the user «calm guide, no rush, no sell». Users resolve the dissonance by trusting the button (visual is faster than prose), and the brand quietly degrades from «considered second pair of eyes» to «another fintech app pushing me to act».

The Lane A trust gradient demands the CTA color **slow the user down**, not speed them up. Sage at #3F5D4A on a cream background does that — it says «considered next step». Mercury's primary CTA solves this exact problem in this exact way.

**Where does oxidized terracotta come from, and why isn't it the primary?**

Stripe Press uses one warm accent per book — sometimes oxblood, sometimes deep coral. Anthropic uses #D97757 coral *sparingly* (citation moments, brand-world shots, never as primary CTA). Craig Mod uses muted red for emphasis. The pattern is: **a warm secondary, used at <5% surface area, for moments that need to be felt rather than read.**

For Provedo, oxidized terracotta #B8704D is exactly the «pen mark» drift indicator and citation-chip color the tactile-shift review described as a localized depth moment. It is NOT a brand color you see on the homepage chrome; it appears at moments of human warmth (a citation appearing, the «drift» pen-mark, a milestone). This is what gives the palette a heartbeat without compromising the editorial cream-and-sage discipline.

## Section 5 — Recommendation rationale

**One palette:**
- BG: #F4F1EA (warm cream, paper-coded)
- Card: #FAF7F0 (lifted cream)
- Ink: #1A1A1A (deep ink, not pure black)
- Accent primary: #3F5D4A (deep sage / forest)
- Accent secondary, sparing: #B8704D (oxidized terracotta)

**Rationale grounded in the four filters:**

1. **Archetype amplification.** Magician+Sage primary lives in cream-paper-and-ink palettes (Stripe Press, Anthropic, Are.na, Granola, Craig Mod, Patagonia). Forest accent specifically carries Sage-intellectualism (Granola, Mercury, Patagonia). Oxidized terracotta secondary carries Magician's «moment of warmth» (Anthropic, Stripe Press, Craig Mod). This palette amplifies all three archetype layers simultaneously; option B (pure white + money-green) amplifies only the Magician-Hero blend that Provedo doesn't claim.

2. **Competitor territory unowned-or-defensible.** Warm cream in fintech is near-empty (Mercury near-white, Origin cream-leaning-mixed). Sage in fintech is lightly populated (Mercury, Origin) — defensible. The combination warm-cream-plus-deep-sage is unoccupied. Adjacent-but-non-competing brands cluster around it (Stripe Press, Granola, Patagonia, Are.na) — trust transfer without competitive overlap. Option B (pure white + money-green) drops Provedo into the busiest fintech palette territory — Stripe, Brex, Mercury (old), Robinhood, Cash App, Plaid all live there.

3. **Voice composition cleanness.** Every voice reference (Patagonia, Craig Mod, Wirecutter, Economist, McPhee) is paper-coded; four out of five are warm-paper-coded specifically. Cream + ink + sage is the *literal* palette of the Patagonia and Craig Mod surfaces the voice doc draws from. Voice and palette become one system instead of two systems fighting. Option B forces voice and palette to disagree on every surface.

4. **Lane A trust signaling.** Sage at #3F5D4A is the «slow down, consider, here's what's there» color. It mirrors Mercury's deliberate 2022 move out of money-green and into sage as a Lane-A trust signal. Money-green at #00875A on primary CTAs reads as «trade now», directly contradicting the «information not advice» promise.

**Why C and not A:**
A (paper-white + sage #5C7A66) is close to the recommendation but lighter. The two adjustments:
- BG #F4F1EA reads warmer and more paper-coded than #FAFAF7 — the Patagonia / Craig Mod cream, not the Anthropic off-white.
- Sage #3F5D4A is deeper and bluer than #5C7A66 — reads forested-considered instead of hospital-mint. At #5C7A66 the sage starts to drift toward soft / generic; at #3F5D4A it sits clearly in the considered-forest band.

Option C as written (warm-white #F9F8F4 + olive #4A6741) is closer in structure but the olive is one undertone too yellow. The recommendation is C-tightened: same warmth philosophy, sharper green.

## Risks

- **«Too quiet for AI»** — readers who expect AI products to look like Vercel/Linear may read this palette as «not techy enough». Mitigation: the type system, motion (count-up, line-draw), and chat-surface treatment can carry the AI-modernity work that the palette deliberately doesn't. Granola is proof this works.
- **Cream printing inconsistency** across screens. F4F1EA on poorly calibrated displays will read either gray or yellowish. Mitigation: spec a slightly cooler fallback (#F2EFE7) that holds across uncalibrated panels.
- **Sage-tinted dark mode is hard** — deep sage on charcoal is the inversion of the cream surface and can read murky. Mitigation: dark mode is a separate spec; do not assume this palette inverts mechanically. PO has not asked for dark mode; defer.
- **Terracotta secondary risks reading «autumn / Thanksgiving»** if used for more than 5% surface. Mitigation: usage rules in the brand doc — terracotta only on citation chips, drift pen-mark, milestone moments. Never as a CTA, never as ambient.
- **«Not bright enough for first-screen pop»** is the inverse of (1) — same response. Hierarchy carries the work; brightness is not the only conversion lever.

## Alternatives

1. **A-as-stated (paper-white #FAFAF7 + sage #5C7A66)** if PO finds C-tightened too warm. Loses some «paper» voice resonance, gains some «AI-tooling» legibility. Acceptable second choice.
2. **B with money-green demoted to data-only** (gain/loss indicators, charts) and primary CTA in deep sage instead. Salvages the Vercel-clean read PO might want without putting money-green on the trust-critical CTA. Acceptable third choice — but at this point you're 80% of the way to the recommendation already.
3. **Stripe Press exact-match palette** (cream #F6F1E8 / oxblood #6B2C2C as accent / ink). Different archetype balance — pulls more Sage-Ruler than Magician+Sage, more institutional. Useful if PO wants more «editorial publishing house» and less «AI knowledge tool». Costs the AI-modernity legibility.
4. **Anthropic-coded** (#FAFAF7 + coral #D97757 + charcoal). Drops sage entirely; centers warmth via coral. Loses the Mercury-trajectory Lane-A signal. Reads more like an AI lab than a finance tool. Not recommended for Provedo's category position, but documented for completeness.
