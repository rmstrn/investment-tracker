# Validation: Warm Tactile UI Direction — Brand-Strategist

**Verdict:** WARN
**Confidence:** high

## Summary

Warm tactile / soft-3D direction is partially compatible with Provedo's brand, but only at low intensity. Magician+Sage primary archetype tolerates warmth and material identity, yet recoils from heavy double-shadow neumorphism — Sage in particular reads as elegant restraint (FT, Stripe Press, The Economist), not as «touchy-feely consumer fintech». The locked voice («observant, composed, plain-spoken», a guide-not-coach posture, Patagonia + Craig Mod + Wirecutter as voice references) explicitly rejects «cheerleader / consumer-app» visual register. Heavy 3D in finance reads as toy (Robinhood, Cash App, Acorns confetti) — exactly the anti-voices the voice doc names by hand. PO's instinct toward tactility is right; the brand's existing «warm cream paper + ink + teal pen-mark» direction is already a tactile system (paper grain, ink, pen). The honest move is to **deepen the existing material metaphor**, not to import a different one. Recommend option **(c) hybrid**, but tighter than PO is sketching: tactile-as-restraint, depth only on agentic moments, never as ambient surface treatment.

## Section 1 — Archetype fit

Provedo's archetype is **Magician + Sage primary · Everyman modifier**. Each axis reads tactility differently:

- **Magician** *(quietly inventive, «wow with sources»)*. Compatible with material identity — paper, ink, pen are magician-coded objects (the magician's tools are physical, not glassy/digital). Compatible with subtle depth (something appearing on paper). **Incompatible with heavy 3D** because heavy double-shadows read as «mechanical / device / gadget», not as quiet invention. Magician's tactility is *artisanal*, not *manufactured*.

- **Sage** *(knows, doesn't preach — Notion / Obsidian / Linear / FT intonation)*. This is the axis at risk. Sage visual register across category: Stripe Press editorial typography, FT printed page, Are.na editorial scrolly, The Economist column rhythm, Anthropic restrained palette. **None of these uses heavy depth.** Sage trades on hierarchy through scale and typography, not through z-axis layering. Heavy neumorphism actively breaks Sage by injecting consumer-app material vocabulary into an editorial register. Moderate depth (single soft shadow, paper grain) does not break Sage; heavy double-shadow neumorphism does.

- **Everyman modifier** *(accessible, warm, not corporate-cold)*. This is the axis that *wants* tactility. Everyman explains why we have warm cream and a teal pen instead of stark white-on-black Mercury minimalism. Everyman is also where PO's instinct lives: «pощупать можно» is an Everyman impulse. The tension: Everyman pushes toward tactility, Sage pulls toward editorial restraint, and Magician asks for invention not gadgetry.

**Synthesis:** the archetype mix can carry *material* tactility (paper, grain, ink, pen, ledger fold-lines) but cannot carry *device* tactility (raised buttons, embossed cards, double-shadow chiclets). The two readings of «tactile» are different brand worlds. The first is editorial; the second is consumer-app.

## Section 2 — Voice consistency

The locked voice doc is unusually explicit about anti-voices: «not Stripe-imperative, not Robinhood-celebratory, not Bloomberg-omniscient, not Granola-precious». The Robinhood line is the relevant one — Robinhood is the canonical heavy-tactile fintech UI (rounded green chiclets, gamified celebration cards, embossed badges). The voice doc names this anti-voice by hand. Heavy 3D would create a direct surface-vs-voice contradiction: prose that sounds like Patagonia + Craig Mod inside a UI shell that looks like Robinhood + Cash App.

The voice references that *do* compose with tactile-as-material: **Patagonia product pages** (paper-tag aesthetic, declarative type), **Craig Mod's newsletter** (printed-page warmth, paper-mail metaphor), **The Economist** (printed-paper roots), **John McPhee** (printed-paper roots), **Wirecutter** (utilitarian-warm review register). Every voice reference points at *paper*, not at *plastic*. That is the strongest single signal in the brief.

Cognitive dissonance is real if PO goes heavy: the user reads «Provedo reads every broker — Interactive Brokers, Schwab, Tinkoff, the small one you opened in 2019 and forgot» (Sample A in voice doc) inside a Cash-App-style raised tile, and the surface tells them «we are a casual money toy» while the prose tells them «we are a calm guide». Two messages, opposite valence, voice loses.

Moderate / hybrid intensity does not create this dissonance because moderate depth reads as *paper layering* (one sheet on top of another sheet), not as *plastic component*.

## Section 3 — Category positioning risk

Provedo's category claim is «portfolio answer engine», sitting between Perplexity (clean-intellectual) and Bloomberg (dense-professional). Both anchor competitors are visually flat. Concrete reference patterns:

| Visual register | Examples | Reading |
|---|---|---|
| Editorial-flat (paper + type) | FT, Stripe Press, The Economist, Are.na, Anthropic | «authoritative, calm, takes itself seriously» |
| Editorial-flat with one accent | Mercury, Linear, Origin, Granola | «modern utility, restrained craft» |
| Soft-card depth | Stripe Dashboard, Notion, Apple Numbers | «professional tool, friendly to use» |
| Heavy 3D / neumorphic | Cash App, Robinhood, Acorns, Daffy | «consumer fintech, casual money, gamified» |
| Skeumorphic-finance | Acorns acorn, old Mint UI | «approachable but unserious» |

The first three rows are where Sage-archetype answer-engines live. The bottom two rows are consumer-fintech B2C territory — exactly the «AI that picks stocks for you» / «pretty dashboard» anti-positioning that the brand-strategist landing doc rejects by hand (sections 8.1 and 8.3 of `2026-04-27-landing-from-scratch-brand-strategist.md`).

**Specific positioning risk if PO goes (a) heavy:** Provedo will read as «AI Robinhood» rather than as «Perplexity for your portfolio». The wedge ICP — the Researcher-Investor reading 10-Ks and listening to earnings calls — is exactly the segment that bounces from Robinhood-coded UI on first impression because it signals «not for me, this is for first-time investors». That is the wrong filter. The ICP wants to feel like the product respects their seriousness; heavy tactility undermines that read.

«Warm artisan instrument» is achievable, but not via neumorphism. The artisan-instrument register is what Stripe Press, Are.na, and Granola actually do — *paper-and-ink editorial with soft single shadows on functional cards*, never *raised plastic chiclets*. Granola is the closest reference: it has cards, soft shadows, warmth, and tactility, but no double-shadows and no plastic vocabulary. Granola is option (b) / (c) — not (a).

## Section 4 — Lane A trust signal

Lane A *information not advice* makes trust the entire moat. Concrete depth-vs-trust patterns from finance UI history:

- **Bloomberg Terminal** — flat, dense, monospace, near-zero depth. Reads as professional and serious. Heavy depth would actively destroy its credibility.
- **Stripe Dashboard** — single soft shadow on cards, restrained. Reads as professional and approachable. Stripe deliberately restrained tactility once it scaled past payments-startup era.
- **Apple Numbers** — flat sheets with single light shadow on toolbars. Tabular figures, monospace data. Tactility lives in the *interaction* (drag handles), not in the *ambient surface*.
- **Cash App / Robinhood** — heavy double-shadow chiclets, rounded corners, gradient buttons, celebration motion. Reads as casual money toy. Both are explicitly low-trust for portfolio-scale work — users move serious assets out of them as portfolios grow.
- **Acorns / Daffy** — skeumorphic acorn-jar, mascot-tactile. Reads as «my first investing app». Wrong vibe.

The pattern is consistent: **as the financial product gets more serious, the surface gets flatter, and tactility migrates from ambient surface treatment to interaction-specific moments** (drag handles, scrubbers, count-up animations on numbers, citation chip reveals). Provedo's existing landing-v2 design system (Granola pattern: cream paper + slate ink + teal pen + paper grain + count-up animations + line-draw reveals) is *already* doing tactile-as-interaction correctly. The question is whether to add more, not whether to start from zero.

Heavy 3D would land Provedo on the wrong side of the trust gradient at exactly the moment Lane A asks the visual to do trust work the legal copy can't. That is the highest-risk single dimension of this decision.

## Section 5 — Recommendation on (a)/(b)/(c)

**Recommend (c) hybrid, in a tighter form than PO is sketching.** Specifically:

- **Baseline (95% of surface area): MODERATE — option (b) intensity.** Single soft shadow on functional cards. Paper grain (already in landing-v2). One teal accent. Editorial typography hierarchy. This is what Granola, Stripe Press, and Origin do, and it is already where the brand wants to be.

- **Localized depth on agentic / tactile moments only:** the *interaction* moments where tactility carries meaning, not where it is decoration.
  - Citation chip reveal (depth signals «receipt produced»)
  - Drag handles on portfolio cards (depth signals «grabbable»)
  - AI reply bubble appearance (subtle lift signals «something arrived»)
  - Primary CTA hover (ink-fill or soft press, signals «ready to commit»)
  - Drift indicator (the «pen mark» — already in landing-v2, deepen slightly)

- **Never apply heavy double-shadow as ambient treatment** to dashboard tiles, navigation, list items, body cards, or chrome. That is the (a) failure mode.

This (c-tight) reading reframes PO's instinct: «pощупать можно» is honored *on the moments where touch matters in the product narrative*, not as a global aesthetic. The result is a UI that feels material and warm without becoming consumer-fintech-coded.

I would not retire landing-v2 «Ledger That Talks». Landing-v2 is already option (b) executed cleanly (warm cream + paper grain + ink + pen + soft cards). The honest move is to *carry the same system into the app* (`/dashboard`, `/positions`, `/chat`, `/insights`), deepening it slightly on agentic moments — not to start a separate visual world for the app and reset the landing.

If PO finds landing-v2 too restrained for personal taste *after* seeing this analysis, the right next step is a side-by-side prototype: same hero in (b-current), (c-tight), and (a-heavy), reviewed against ICP visual archetype testing — not a directional pivot from one cold reference image.

## Risks

- **Sage-archetype erosion** if depth goes ambient. Sage is the trust axis; eroding it costs us against Bloomberg / Perplexity benchmark.
- **Voice-vs-surface contradiction** with Patagonia / Craig Mod / Wirecutter prose inside Cash-App-shaped chrome. Visible to the user as «something feels off», invisible to the team that built it.
- **Wedge ICP mis-filtering.** Researcher-Investor bounces on Robinhood-coded UI in <3 seconds. Wrong segment self-filters in.
- **Sunk-cost loss** of landing-v2 work if PO retires PR #66 / PR #65 for a direction the brand can't carry. Zero of the brand-strategist + brand-voice + designer landing analysis points at heavy neumorphism; reversing direction without adding new evidence costs ~3 weeks of compounded work.
- **Domain saturation.** Heavy neumorphism peaked aesthetically ~2020 (UI dribble era) and has aged badly in serious-product categories. 2026 fintech consensus has moved back toward editorial-flat (FT, Mercury, Stripe, Anthropic, Granola). Going heavy now is fashion-late as well as brand-wrong.

## Alternatives (if WARN/REJECT)

1. **Deepen the existing paper metaphor in-place.** Add: subtle ink-bleed on hover states, page-fold shadows on card edges (single soft shadow at 4-6px y-offset, never double), cursor-as-pen on interactive surfaces, paper-tear divider variant on section breaks, slight warm vignette on full-bleed sections. Honors PO's tactile instinct without changing visual world.

2. **Tactile-as-interaction, not tactile-as-surface.** Spec a small set of «touch moments» (citation chip, drag handle, CTA press, AI reply lift, drift pen-mark) where depth is allowed to be more pronounced. Everywhere else stays flat-editorial. Document the rule in `04_DESIGN_BRIEF.md` so it survives the next reset impulse.

3. **Run a one-week prototype before committing.** Build the dashboard hero card in three intensities — current landing-v2 (b), (c-tight as recommended above), and one heavier variant — then walk PO through them next to FT, Stripe Press, Granola, Robinhood, and Cash App for comparison. Decision becomes evidence-backed rather than reference-image-driven. Cheap insurance against a 3-week reset.

4. **If PO still wants heavy after the prototype:** the smallest-blast-radius scope is *paywall surfaces and onboarding stages* — moments where consumer-fintech vocabulary actually fits the funnel work. Keep the dashboard, chat, insights, and landing in editorial-flat. This is a compromise scope, not a recommendation.
